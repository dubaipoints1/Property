## The Instagram question — official council position

The 20-second attention span is the **right framing** but the **wrong fear**.

Points-and-miles is a high-intent, search-led category. Nobody opens
Instagram thinking *should I move my AED 25k salary to FAB or ENBD for
the welcome bonus*. They Google it.

Brand position (six-month target):

> The default second tab a UAE reader opens — after the bank's own page
> — when they're about to commit five figures to a card, a salary
> transfer, or a redemption.

Build for the open tab. Not the scroll.

## UAE social-media compliance constraint (operator addendum)

Under UAE National Media Council rules:

- **Branded social presence** (Instagram / LinkedIn page / TikTok in the
  publication's name) requires an **eMedia licence** — approximately
  AED 15,000 / year + paperwork.
- **Influencer permits** are required for any paid placement.
- **Cybercrime Law** makes false / unsubstantiated comparison claims
  actionable. The site's "AED-first, source-verified, dated" fence is
  protective here, but social posts that strip context are riskier.

In-scope without licensing:

- The website itself.
- Email newsletter (Buttondown).
- Personal-list WhatsApp broadcast under 256 subscribers.
- Operator's personal LinkedIn commenting / sharing.

Out of scope until licensing decision:

- A branded Instagram, TikTok, X / Twitter, YouTube account.
- A branded LinkedIn company page.
- WhatsApp broadcast scaled past 256 subs (commercial threshold).

**Implication for M4 distribution** below: ship newsletter + WhatsApp
+ SEO first; defer branded social until the operator commits to the
licensing path.
