## Page-type purpose

A how-to / explainer guide. Differs from a card review or trip review.

## Article scaffold (in document order)

1. **Comments-icon + share buttons** at top (same chrome as card review)
2. **Lede paragraph** — sets up the question the guide answers
3. **`In this post:`** literal text (presumably renders a jump-link TOC client-side, not captured in markdown)
4. **`## What is X?`** H2 — definitional opener
5. **`## Which routes have X?`** H2 with H3 sub-sections
6. **`## Which planes have X?`** H2 with H3 sub-sections per aircraft type
7. **`## Qatar Airways has stopped reconfiguring planes`** H2 — **opinion-laden, classic OMAAT**
8. **`## Bottom line`** H2

## Voice signals — opinion-laden H2s

> ## Qatar Airways has stopped reconfiguring planes
>
> Qatar Airways appears to have basically given up on reconfiguring aircraft with Qsuites. By my math, in the past few years, we've seen a grand total of two 777s reconfigured with Qsuites, and that's it.

H2 makes a strong editorial claim ("has stopped"); body backs it with author's math. This is OMAAT's signature pattern — guides are NOT neutral SEO documents, they carry opinion.

## Implications for dubaipoints.ae /guides/ template

1. **No call-to-action expected** — guides don't push a single product. Don't try to retrofit one.
2. **Question-form H2s + opinion-form H2s mix** — "What is X?" + "Which routes have X?" pattern is SEO-friendly; OMAAT mixes in editorial H2s like "X has stopped doing Y" as the editorial signature.
3. **Same image-caption discipline as card reviews** — already in our Tier-A adopt list.
4. **Bottom Line H2 always** — consistent across OMAAT page types.
5. **No card-spec chrome on guides** — keeps the page focused on the explanation.
6. **Comments + share UI is layout-level**, not page-specific — fits OMAAT's site-wide comment system.
