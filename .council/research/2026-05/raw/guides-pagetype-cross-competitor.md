# Guide page-type — cross-competitor structural notes
_Scraped 2026-05-22 — 1 sample per competitor for the "how-to guide" page type._

## TPG — "Chase Ultimate Rewards sweet spots: From international business class to domestic travel"
_Source: https://thepointsguy.com/loyalty-programs/sweet-spots-chase-ultimate-rewards/_
_Author: Ben Smithson + Benét J. Wilson (multi-byline) · 8 min read · Modified 2026-04-30_

Scaffold:
1. Breadcrumb (`Guides`)
2. H1 + multi-byline panel (2 authors with photos + bios)
3. Date + read-time
4. Featured image (full-width, photo credit beneath)
5. **Jump to section** sticky pill
6. Compensation disclosure paragraph
7. **"Quick summary" callout** — bulleted highlights, sub-section "What to consider", sub-section "What you'll miss from the article" with "Generated by AI with support from our editorial team" attribution
8. **Lede** (2 paragraphs)
9. **Sequential `## Book X with Y` H2s** — one H2 per sweet-spot redemption (6 H2s)
10. Each H2 has: photo + uppercase credit + body paragraphs + Related: link
11. Newsletter signup block mid-article
12. CardMatch tool inserted mid-article
13. `## How to earn Chase Ultimate Rewards points` H2 with bulleted card list (each card a tracking-URL Apply link)
14. `## Bottom line` H2
15. Featured image credit
16. Editorial disclaimer
17. Comments (Beta)
18. `## Check out current card offers` — 5 sponsored card strip
19. Related stories (4 thumbnails)
20. **TPG featured card sidebar** — full card spec including rewards-rate table, intro offer, annual fee, APR, credit recommended, pros/cons, full benefits bulleted list, Apply CTA

Notable: **AI-summary callout** at the top labels itself explicitly ("Generated by AI with support from our editorial team"). Most editorial publication on the dossier-set; pre-empts the "did AI write this?" question.

## HfP — "What is the best use of American Express Membership Rewards points?"
_Source: https://www.headforpoints.com/2025/10/29/what-is-the-best-use-of-amex-membership-rewards-points-2/_
_Author: Rob Burgess · Modified 2025-10-29 · 26 comments (closed to new comments)_

Scaffold:
1. **Date + byline + comment-count + category + share** (same chrome as card review)
2. **Lede** — bolded question + setup paragraphs
3. **Repeating special-offer callouts** (2 of them, bolded, with Apply links — same pattern as HfP card reviews)
4. **Card-thumbnail mini-cards inline** for each Amex card discussed (image + name + 1-line value-prop + "Read our full review" link)
5. `## How can you earn Membership Rewards points?` H2 (question form)
6. `## How we value Amex Membership Rewards points:` H2
7. **`### NN.NNp per point (target/guaranteed) – [redemption type]` H3 pattern repeated** — 9 H3 sub-sections, one per redemption type
8. Each H3 carries: a stated value-per-point + reasoning + transfer ratio + link to fuller article on the underlying programme
9. `## Conclusion` H2 — short, 2 paragraphs
10. **Newsletter subscribe CTA** mid-article (HfP style — "**To join our 80,000 free subscribers, click the button below**")
11. **"Want to earn more points from credit cards?" footer block** with 4 more card mini-cards
12. **Small business cards block** with 3 more card mini-cards
13. Category tags
14. **Comments (closed for new comments — points to forum instead)**

Notable: **HfP's signature `Value to me: £X` pattern adapted to guide page-type** as `NN.NNp per point (target/guaranteed)`. Same structural move (per-row valuation with explicit reasoning) but applied to redemption options rather than card benefits. Reusable convention.

Also: **"This article is closed to new comments. Feel free to ask your question in the HfP forums."** — HfP migrates discussion to forums after a window. Curation play.

## OMAAT — "Which Qatar Airways Planes & Flights Have Qsuites?" (covered separately at `raw/omaat-guide-qsuites.md`)

Key contrasts with TPG / HfP:
- **No callouts** (no "Hot Tip", no "Quick summary"). Pure prose.
- **Opinion-laden H2s** ("Qatar Airways has stopped reconfiguring planes")
- **No AI-summary callout** ("no ghostwriters or AI at OMAAT" is OMAAT's brand stance)
- **Per-image inline captions** on every image
- **Comments fully threaded** at bottom with Most Recent / Most Helpful sort

## Implications for dubaipoints.ae /guides/

1. **The TPG "Quick summary" + AI-disclosure block** is worth flagging — Charter doesn't yet take a position on AI-written content, but if we ever use AI assist, this is how to surface it.
2. **HfP's per-redemption-row value math** (`NN.NNp per point (target/guaranteed)`) directly maps to our airline / loyalty-programme pages. Adopt for `/airlines/skywards/` and similar.
3. **OMAAT's per-image caption discipline** continues to be the cleanest pattern. Already Tier A in the dossier.
4. **Card-thumbnail mini-cards inline** (HfP) for related cards mentioned in guide prose — useful pattern for our `/guides/` referencing specific cards in our card collection.
5. **No card-specific chrome on guides** — guides should be free of the AtAGlance / EditorVerdict / EditorScorecard chrome that's correct on `/cards/<slug>/`.
6. **Bottom Line H2 universal** across guide page-type — confirms our Phase 2a.2.5 pattern adoption is correct for all editorial page types, not just card reviews.
