## "Value to me" pattern (HfP's signature move)

Under H2 "core benefits", each benefit is an H3, followed by:
- Description of the benefit (1–3 short paragraphs)
- A separator line: **"Value to me: £X"** with explicit reasoning

Sample sequence:

> ### £200 of UK dining credit:
> **You will receive £200 cashback per year when eating at 160+ UK restaurants listed here.**
> This is split into £100 per half year. There is no small print – you don't need to book via any special link or quote any code. You simply register for the offer and then pay on The Platinum Card when you dine.
> **Value to me:** £200. We eat in some of the participating restaurants on a regular basis so this is essentially free money for me.

Conclusion then sums these up:

> Looking at the maths above, **I get £1,500 of 'value' per year from The Platinum Card, which is double the £650 annual fee.**

This pattern turns a benefits list into a transparent value calculation. **Reader can do the same math for themselves with different inputs.** Editorial honesty signal — "I rarely use Priority Pass because I have BA status" — admits non-universal value.
