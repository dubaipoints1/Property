# Competitor page-type structural matrix

_Dossier addendum — opened 2026-05-22 by Head of Research. Follow-on to `.council/research/2026-05/competitor-teardown.md` v2 and `.council/research/2026-05/fab-elite-delta.md`._

**Purpose:** Per user direction — "take a sample of each [page type] per competitor to see how it looks so we can apply it structurally to our site." This addendum maps each competitor's page-type templates against dubaipoints.ae's planned page-type set, producing a per-page-type structural recipe for the propagation work.

**Method:** Firecrawl scrapes of one representative sample per page type per competitor. ~21 Firecrawl scrapes total across the dossier work to date.

## Coverage matrix

| Page type | TPG | UP | OMAAT | HfP | Dubaipoints equivalent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Homepage | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | `/` |
| Card review | ✅ ×2 | ✅ ×6 | ✅ | ✅ | `/cards/<slug>/` |
| Guide / how-to | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | `/guides/<slug>/` |
| Comparison (X vs Y) | ✅ | ❌ (links inline) | ❌ (links inline) | ❌ (inline in reviews) | `<CardComparison>` component on card pages |
| Listicle / "Best X" | ✅ | (similar in template) | ❌ (single voice) | ❌ (inline weekly digest) | `/cards/best-X/` (future) |
| Loyalty programme review | (links to other content) | ✅ | (linked to programme pages) | ✅ | `/airlines/<slug>/` |
| Deal post / time-limited | (news section) | (news section) | (deal posts on homepage) | ✅ | `/deals/<slug>/` |
| Trip report | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ multi-part | ❌ | (out of scope) |

**Note on partial coverage:** UP's comparison + listicle + loyalty-programme pages all use the same scaffold as their card review (confirmed empirically — same trust block wording, same methodology footer). They have one master template that morphs to fit content type. OMAAT and HfP weave comparisons inline into reviews rather than running standalone vs pages. The matrix gaps are mostly structural ("doesn't exist as a separate page") rather than "wasn't sampled."

## Per-page-type structural recipes

### Page-type A — Homepage
_Covered in detail in `.council/research/2026-05/competitor-teardown.md` v2 §"Homepage architecture"._

**One-line recipe per competitor:**
- **TPG**: Quick-link tile strip at top (TSA wait times, Top credit cards, Newsletter, etc.) — opinionated navigation, no editorial above the fold
- **UP**: Hero ("Our Readers Travel Like They Never Thought Possible") → guide categories → news → travel hub → **trust footer** (As seen in / Meet the Team / How We Work / Editorial Disclosure)
- **OMAAT**: News-led river, single byline (Ben Schlappig), **stats-brag footer** ("Miles Traveled / Words Written / Posts Published")
- **HfP**: News-led river with **product-anchored clusters** ("The Platinum Card®" / "Amex Gold" subsections) interleaved

**Dubaipoints implication:** Currently a flat directory. Tier-B actions named in dossier — trust-stack (UP), quick-link tiles (TPG), product clusters (HfP). All unblocked.

### Page-type B — Card review
_Covered in detail in `.council/research/2026-05/competitor-teardown.md` v2 §"Card-review template" + `.council/research/2026-05/raw/upgradedpoints-template-consistency-test.md` for empirical UP consistency proof._

**Dubaipoints implication:** Template now established (Phases 2a.2.0–2a.2.7). Worked examples on Skywards Infinite + Signature + fab-elite + fab-cashback. Phase 2a.2.1b propagation across remaining 27 cards in progress.

### Page-type C — Guide / how-to
_Detailed cross-competitor notes in `.council/research/2026-05/raw/guides-pagetype-cross-competitor.md`._

**Master recipe for `/guides/<slug>/`:**

1. Breadcrumb (`Guides / <category>`)
2. H1 + byline (single or multi)
3. Date + read-time
4. Featured image with credit
5. **Jump-to-section sticky pill** (TPG signature — adopted in Phase 2a.2.7 via layout integration; works on guides automatically once they have H2s)
6. **`### Quick summary` callout** (TPG pattern) with: bulleted highlights, "What to consider" sub-block, optional "Generated by AI" disclosure
7. Lede (1–2 paragraphs)
8. Sequential H2s (one per main idea, question-form OR opinion-form)
9. **Per-row value math** for redemption guides (HfP signature — `0.55p per point (target/guaranteed) – Club Eurostar` format)
10. Per-image inline caption (OMAAT signature)
11. Newsletter signup mid-article (TPG / HfP pattern)
12. `## Bottom line` H2 (universal)
13. Featured-card sidebar (TPG) OR card-thumbnail mini-cards inline (HfP)
14. Comments (HfP / OMAAT — threaded; TPG — Beta)
15. Related-articles strip at foot

**Dubaipoints implication:** We have a `/guides/` collection in `src/content.config.ts` but no template yet. This recipe maps directly. Worth a Phase 2a.3 brief.

### Page-type D — Comparison ("X vs Y")
_Sample: TPG "Amex Platinum vs Chase Sapphire Reserve" at `raw/tpg-comparison-amex-plat-vs-csr.json` (54 KB)_

**TPG comparison-page recipe** (other competitors weave comparisons inline rather than as standalone pages):

1. H1 ("Card X vs Card Y: Which is right for you?")
2. Multi-byline (matches card-review chrome)
3. Intro setting up the comparison
4. **Side-by-side fact-tile strip** — both cards' fees / earn / bonus aligned
5. H2 sections by dimension (Welcome bonus / Earn rates / Lounge access / Travel protections / etc.)
6. Each H2 contains both cards' info, often as a 2-col table or paired bulleted lists
7. **"When card X wins" / "When card Y wins"** decision-helper H2s
8. Bottom Line picking one (or "depends on your spending pattern")

**Dubaipoints implication:** We have `<CardComparison>` component for inline use. A standalone `/compare/<slug-a>-vs-<slug-b>/` route would be a Phase 2a.4 brief. Demand-validated once we have analytics.

### Page-type E — Listicle / "Best X"
_Sample: TPG "Best Premium Travel Cards" at `raw/tpg-listicle-best-premium-travel-cards.json` (88 KB)_

**TPG listicle recipe:**

1. H1 ("Best Premium Travel Credit Cards of 2026")
2. Multi-byline (matches card-review chrome)
3. Date + read-time
4. Featured image
5. Jump-to-section sticky pill (each listed card is an H2 anchor)
6. **"Best for X" rubric** at the top with 8–12 sub-categories ("Best overall" / "Best for lounge access" / "Best for dining" / etc.)
7. Per-card H2 section (each repeating the card-spec mini-card: name + image + rating + Apply CTA + 3-5 pros + 3-5 cons + "Why we like it" prose paragraph)
8. **`## How we chose the best premium cards`** — methodology section, universal across listicles
9. `## How to choose the right premium card for you` — decision framework
10. `## Bottom line` H2

**Dubaipoints implication:** Maps directly to our `/cards/` directory index page. Charter §10 forbids "best X" claims without basis — adapt the rubric to "Editor's pick / Strong / Solid / Niche" tiers (which is our existing `tier` frontmatter). Worth a Phase 2a.5 brief.

### Page-type F — Loyalty programme review
_Sample: UP "Chase Ultimate Rewards Review" at `raw/upgradedpoints-loyalty-chase-ur-review.json` (82 KB)_

UP's loyalty-programme review uses the same scaffold as their card reviews (because the "Card Details" / "Pros & Cons" / "Great Card If / Don't Get If" structure is a master template, not a card-specific shape). What changes:
- Replace "U.P. Rating" star block with a programme-rating score (or omit)
- Replace "Welcome Bonus" with "How to earn points"
- Replace "Earn rates table" with transfer-partner table

**Dubaipoints implication:** Our existing `/airlines/skywards/` page is a loyalty-programme review in this sense. Adapting the canonical template (KeyTakeaways + AtAGlance-equivalent + GreatCardIf-equivalent for "Who is Skywards for?") is a Phase 2a.6 brief.

### Page-type G — Deal post / time-limited offer
_Sample: HfP "Use Companion Voucher with BA Holidays" — short news-style article._

**HfP deal-post recipe:**

1. Date + byline + comment-count + category + share (same chrome as card review)
2. **Bold lede** stating the news ("British Airways has launched a new way to redeem your...")
3. Body explains the offer mechanics in plain prose
4. **`## How does it work?`** H2 — mechanics explained step-by-step
5. **`## What can you do with your Companion Voucher?`** H2 — practical applications
6. **"Here is the small print:"** bulleted list of caveats and dates
7. **`## Is this a good deal?`** H2 — opinionated verdict (HfP signature)
8. Honest framing: "No, not really." — author doesn't fake enthusiasm for weak offers
9. Comments thread (59 comments on this sample — high engagement)

**Dubaipoints implication:** Maps to our `/deals/` collection. The "Is this a good deal?" question-H2 + opinionated answer is the editorial honesty signal Charter §10 mandates. Our deals should not be promotional summaries; they should be opinionated analyses. Phase 2a.7 brief — deal template.

### Page-type H — Trip report (OMAAT only)
_Sample: not scraped; OMAAT homepage shows the genre clearly._

OMAAT is the only competitor running long-form multi-part trip reports as a genre. **Out of scope for dubaipoints.ae** — our audience is points-and-miles utility, not first-person travelogue. Note for future.

## Cross-page-type universal patterns

Patterns that recur across **every page type** at one or more competitors — these are layout-level concerns, not page-template concerns:

1. **Sticky site header with social-share + comments-icon** (OMAAT)
2. **Newsletter signup block** appearing site-wide (HfP, TPG, UP)
3. **Comments + share UI** appearing on every editorial page (HfP, OMAAT)
4. **Trust footer with "Meet the Team" / "How We Work"** (UP — site-wide)
5. **Per-image inline caption** discipline (OMAAT — site-wide policy)
6. **"Bottom line" H2** universal across editorial page types (all four)
7. **Featured / Related card sidebars** at the foot of editorial content (TPG, UP, HfP)

**Dubaipoints implication:** Items 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 are layout-level (BaseLayout / page-specific layout components) — not per-MDX content. Worth a separate Tier B brief to centralise these.
