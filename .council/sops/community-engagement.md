# SOP — Soft-touch community engagement on Reddit and similar forums

**Owner**: Standards Editor.
**Tier**: T1 per contribution; T2 for any new subreddit added to scope.
**Charter constraints**:
- §1 — Dubai-first voice; tone applies on Reddit as on-site.
- §5 — No advertorial-driven recommendations; affiliate disclosure
  rules apply when linking pages with affiliate language.
- §7 — Council sign-off required when a contribution links to a
  page that hasn't yet shipped (the Standards Editor previews the
  link before posting).

## Why this SOP exists

UAE points and miles questions surface on Reddit, X, WhatsApp
groups, and expat Slack communities daily. dubaipoints.ae has
working answers on most of them already. With no SOP, individual
editors are tempted to either (a) ignore the threads and let the
question rot, (b) DM-pitch and risk a self-promotion ban, or
(c) drop unattributed links that read as marketing. None of those
serves the reader or the brand.

The competitor surfaced on `r/UAEcreditcards` (12 May 2026)
demonstrated the failure mode: a solo builder dropping a paid-tool
link into a thread, generating one upvote, fifteen sceptical
comments, and zero conversions. We can do better with discipline.

## When to invoke

- **A reader question lands** in a watched subreddit (list below)
  that an existing dubaipoints.ae article answers directly.
- **A factual error spreads** on a watched community — wrong AED
  figure, wrong eligibility rule, wrong programme mechanic. We
  correct with a primary-source citation, never with a brand pitch.
- **A new section editor onboards** — read this SOP before their
  first contribution.

## Process — single contribution

1. **Identify yourself.** Each editor uses one disclosed Reddit /
   forum account, with a flair or bio that names the role
   (e.g. "Editor, dubaipoints.ae — UAE cards & loyalty"). No
   sock-puppets, no alt accounts, no anonymous endorsements.
2. **Read the full thread first.** Confirm the question hasn't
   already been answered well; confirm you're not stepping over a
   redditor who got it right.
3. **Draft the reply in plain text**, not a marketing pitch:
   - Answer the question directly, in your own words, in two
     paragraphs maximum.
   - Cite the primary source (bank KFS PDF, official airline page)
     wherever a figure or rule appears. Citing dubaipoints.ae alone
     is not enough — link the primary too.
   - Link **one** dubaipoints.ae page only, and only if it adds
     depth beyond what the reply itself contains. Never the
     homepage; always a specific article, hub, or guide.
4. **Standards Editor review.** Any reply that links to
   dubaipoints.ae routes through the Standards Editor in Slack
   before posting. Replies that contain only primary-source
   citations can ship without review.
5. **Log the contribution.** After posting, append a line to
   `.council/community-log/YYYY-MM.md`:
   - Date
   - Editor handle
   - Thread URL
   - One-sentence summary of the question answered
   - Standards Editor sign-off note (if applicable)
6. **Don't follow up.** Post once, answer follow-up questions if
   they arrive, but don't bump the thread, don't ask for upvotes,
   don't DM the OP.

## Subreddits in scope at launch

- `r/UAEcreditcards` — primary target. UAE-specific points and miles
  questions, low volume, high fit.
- `r/dubai` — broad; only contribute when the thread is a money /
  cards / loyalty question, not when it's a "where do I find good
  shawarma" thread.
- `r/UAE` — same scope rule as `r/dubai`.
- `r/expats` — UAE-tagged threads only.

Adding any subreddit not on this list requires Standards Editor
approval and a brief on why the audience fit is worth the
moderation risk. T2 sign-off.

## Cadence

- **≤ 3 contributions per editor per week.** Soft cap. Quality
  beats volume.
- **Standards Editor monthly review.** Each month, sample one
  contribution per editor; flag any that drifted toward promotional
  language for retraining.
- **Quarterly kill-switch review.** Chairman checks the engagement
  log against site analytics; if no measurable reader benefit
  surfaces (newsletter signups attributable to Reddit, organic
  citations of dubaipoints.ae in unrelated threads), the SOP is
  retired.

## Kill-list — what you must never do

- Use superlatives outside what the primary source supports ("best
  card in UAE" is forbidden; "highest cap among the three Skywards
  co-brand cards under AED 700 annual fee, per their published KFS
  schedules" is allowed if true).
- Use affiliate language ("apply via my link", "I get a referral")
  outside the affiliate-disclosed pages on dubaipoints.ae itself.
  Forum replies don't carry affiliate links, ever.
- Brigade or vote-stack. No coordinating upvotes in the editor
  Slack. No asking friends to vote. No alt accounts.
- DM-pitch. Replies stay in the public thread.
- Cross-post the same reply to multiple subreddits. Tailor each
  contribution to the thread.
- Cite dubaipoints.ae as the only source. Always pair our link with
  the primary source the article itself cites.
- Engage with the competitor publicly. No quote-tweets, no thread
  replies on `uaecreditcardmatch.com`'s posts. The fact-check work
  (Workstream C of the May 2026 plan) is internal until Chairman
  authorises any external comment.

## What this SOP deliberately does NOT do

- Authorise paid Reddit ads or sponsored posts. Those route through
  a separate Chairman + Growth Lead decision and are out of scope
  for this SOP.
- Authorise an "official dubaipoints.ae" brand account. Editors
  post under their own disclosed identity, not under a brand handle.
- Cover X / Twitter, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, or Discord. Each platform
  needs its own scope decision; this SOP is Reddit-shaped because
  Reddit is where the relevant questions cluster.
- Replace the Chairman's gate on press response or crisis comms.
  Any thread that touches a regulatory, legal, or reputational
  question escalates to the Chairman immediately and the editor
  does **not** post a reply until the Chairman has signed off.

## Reference — first three contributions

When this SOP first ships, the Standards Editor seeds the log with
three reference contributions to demonstrate the shape:

1. A `r/UAEcreditcards` thread asking which card maximises Skywards
   on dining — answered with the typed `earnRates.dining` data, a
   link to the relevant card review on dubaipoints.ae, plus the
   bank KFS source.
2. A `r/dubai` thread asking about salary-transfer bonuses —
   answered with the live tracker URL on dubaipoints.ae and the
   bank's own offer page as the primary.
3. A `r/expats` thread asking about credit-card eligibility for new
   UAE residents — answered with the relevant guide on
   dubaipoints.ae and the AECB regulatory page as the primary.

Each reference contribution is logged with Standards Editor sign-off
visible in `.council/community-log/2026-05.md`.
