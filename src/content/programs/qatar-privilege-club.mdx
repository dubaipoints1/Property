Qatar Privilege Club is the loyalty programme of Qatar Airways. It
moved from proprietary Qmiles to **Avios** in 2022, aligning with
the British Airways / Iberia / Aer Lingus / Finnair family — though
the four programmes remain separate with their own award charts and
expiry rules. For UAE residents, Privilege Club is the closest non-
Emirates pathway to a top-tier Business product (Qsuite, ex-DOH, an
hour up the road) and the most flexible loyalty currency in the
region.

## Tier structure

Privilege Club runs four published tiers — **Burgundy**, **Silver**,
**Gold** and **Platinum** — plus the published-but-rarely-seen
**Platinum Plus**. Tier earning is via "Qpoints", a status-currency
separate from Avios and earned only on Qatar / Oneworld paid flights.
Silver matches into Oneworld Ruby; Gold into Oneworld Sapphire
(lounge access plus extra baggage); Platinum into Oneworld Emerald
(First-class lounges, priority on every leg). Gold is the meaningful
tier for most UAE residents who fly Qatar three or four long-hauls a
year.

## Who Qatar Privilege Club suits

Earning into Privilege Club Avios makes sense for a UAE resident in
two profiles:

- **You take long-haul Business once or twice a year and value the
  hard product over the airline.** Qsuite beats Emirates Business and
  Etihad Business on a per-seat-class basis. A 1-hour positioning hop
  to Doha (DXB → DOH is AED 400–600 cash, or a cheap Avios redemption
  in itself) is a low cost to pay for the seat.
- **You want a transferable Oneworld currency.** Avios in Privilege
  Club redeems across BA, Iberia, Cathay, JAL, Qantas, Royal Jordanian
  and others — broader partner reach than Skywards or Guest Miles can
  offer.

## How to earn from the UAE

Direct co-brand: the Mashreq Qatar Privilege Club Visa Infinite and
Visa Signature, currently the only Avios-into-Privilege-Club earners
issued in the UAE. Earn rates cluster around 1.0–2.0 Avios per AED 1
on the card, with multipliers on Qatar-booked airfare and at Qatar
Duty Free.

Indirect: **Marriott Bonvoy → Avios at 3:1 plus 5,000-Avios bonus per
60,000 points transferred** — the single best route into the
currency for most UAE residents, because Bonvoy can be earned through
multiple UAE-issued credit cards and through every paid Marriott
stay in Dubai. Qatar also runs frequent **transfer bonuses** from
hotel programmes (commonly 30–50% on Bonvoy transfers, three or four
times a year) — worth waiting for.

## What to watch

The 2024 Privilege Club refresh introduced **dynamic Avios pricing**
on a subset of routes, particularly peak-season DOH → London and DOH
→ Bangkok. Saver-level pricing still exists on most routes but
clears fast 30 days out. Set up the Qatar award-search alerts before
you transfer Bonvoy points in.

The other watch-item is the **Avios pooling-and-combining mechanic**:
in 2023, BA, Iberia and Qatar enabled the ability to **combine
Avios across the four programmes** — meaning if you hold 30,000 BA
Avios and want to redeem 70,000 on a Qatar award, you can move BA
Avios into Privilege Club at 1:1 to top up. The combining mechanic
is unidirectional in some cases and carries periodic outage windows;
do not rely on it as a same-day fix, and check Qatar's current pooling-and-combining rules across the four Avios programmes before moving a balance.
