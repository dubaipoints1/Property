Etihad Guest is the loyalty programme of Etihad Airways, headquartered
in Abu Dhabi. For UAE residents in Abu Dhabi or the northern emirates
it sits alongside Emirates Skywards as a flag-carrier earning option;
the choice between the two is rarely close once you actually count
your annual segments.

## Tier structure

Etihad Guest runs four published tiers — **Silver**, **Gold**,
**Platinum** and the invitation-only **Platinum Exclusive** — earned
on Tier Miles accrued in a calendar year. Silver gets you priority
check-in and extra baggage; Gold adds lounge access on Etihad metal
and a confirmed companion benefit; Platinum is the meaningful tier,
adding guaranteed Business-class award availability on Etihad metal
and a higher mileage-earning multiplier on paid fares.

## Who Etihad Guest suits

Earning into Guest Miles makes sense for a UAE resident if you fly
Etihad four or more segments a year, *or* if your home is in Abu
Dhabi or Al Ain and DXB is the inconvenient airport. For everyone
else — particularly Dubai-based residents who can walk onto an
Emirates flight to anywhere — Skywards is the default and Guest
Miles is the hedge.

The programme's second use case is as a Marriott Bonvoy transfer
target. Bonvoy points transfer to Etihad Guest at 3:1 with a 5,000-
mile bonus per 60,000 points moved — a useful diversification away
from Skywards if you suspect Emirates is heading toward a quiet
chart devaluation.

## How to earn from the UAE

Direct earn cards in market include the FAB Etihad Guest Premium,
the Emirates NBD Etihad Guest Inspire, and the ADIB Etihad Guest
co-brand suite. Earn rates on co-brand cards typically cluster
around 1.0–2.5 Guest Miles per AED 1 spent on the card, with
elevated multipliers (3–4 Miles per AED 1) on Etihad-booked airfare.

Indirect routes: Marriott Bonvoy transfers (3:1 + 5k per 60k),
Hilton Honors transfers (10:1, which is rarely a good idea on raw
maths), and Accor ALL → Etihad at 4,000:2,000.
