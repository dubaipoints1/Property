## Earn rates

Earn rates are credited as **Skywards Miles per USD 1** of eligible spend (USD 1 ≈ AED 3.67;
2 miles per USD on partner categories works out to roughly 0.54 miles per AED 1):

<EarnRateTable card="emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite" />

Miles post directly to your Emirates Skywards account — no transfer step.

<HotTip>
ENBD's marketing quotes miles per **USD**, not per AED. The 2× partner rate
works out to ~0.54 miles per AED 1 spent — competitive but not as eye-catching
as "2 miles per dollar" reads on the first scan. Compare like-for-like by
converting other cards' AED-quoted rates to USD before benchmarking.
</HotTip>

## What you get — per-benefit value

Per the [2 fils-per-mile baseline](/airlines/skywards/) we use for every
UAE Skywards co-brand review. Add the numbers up against the AED 1,575
year-two fee.

### Welcome bonus

100,000 Skywards Miles on the first AED 100,000 of spend within the
welcome window.
**Value to me: AED 2,000** at the 2-fil baseline — clears the AED 1,575
year-two fee with change, but covers under two-thirds of the AED 3,149
joining fee on its own; the year-one case needs the rest of the
package. A single Y-to-J upgrade redemption can deliver materially
more (see the methodology note on the
[Skywards programme overview](/airlines/skywards/)).

### Visa Airport Companion lounge access

Unlimited cardholder visits + 1 guest at 1,000+ lounges worldwide
including DXB, AUH, LHR. At an AED 200 walk-in equivalent per visit:
**Value to me: AED 2,400/year** for a regular flyer (six trips with
a guest); **AED 800/year** for a two-trip-a-year traveller.

### Year-one Skywards Silver status

Priority check-in, extra baggage allowance, lounge access on Skywards
flights, tier miles toward Gold. We value this at one
free checked bag per trip on flights you'd otherwise pay for.
**Value to me: AED 200–400/year** for two-to-four flown trips. Lost
from year two unless you re-qualify on flown tier miles — so this is
a year-one figure, not perpetual.

### Earn on AED 100,000 of card spend

Base earn is 1 mile per **USD 1** (≈ 0.27 miles per AED 1), so
AED 100,000 of everyday spend earns ~27,000 Miles. At the 2-fil
baseline: **Value to me: roughly AED 550/year** on the base earn —
up to about **AED 1,100/year** if most of your spend lands on the 2×
categories (Emirates, flydubai, dining, Skywards Plus partners),
which earn ~0.54 miles per AED 1.

### Express Miles add-on (optional, AED 250 + 5% VAT/month)

50% more miles on non-Emirates spend. At AED 8,000/month of
non-Emirates spend (≈ USD 2,180), the lift is ~1,090 extra miles a
month — about AED 22 of value at the baseline, against AED 262.50 of
monthly cost. **Value to me: clearly negative.** Break-even at the
2-fil baseline needs roughly AED 96,000 of non-Emirates spend per
month; skip it unless you consistently redeem far above baseline.

For a UAE reader clearing AED 100,000+ on the card who flies four
trips a year, year one sums to roughly **AED 4,000–4,500** at the
baseline (welcome + lounge + Silver + earn) against the AED 3,149
joining fee — comfortably positive, and materially more if the
welcome bonus lands on a Y-to-J upgrade. From year two the package
is lounge plus earn: roughly **AED 2,000–2,700** against the
AED 1,575 renewal. Positive, but it is the lounge access doing the
work, not the earn rate.

## Key conditions to know

<dl class="dp-conditions">
  <dt>Lounge access</dt>
  <dd>Visa Airport Companion App, not Priority Pass. Unlimited at 1,000+ lounges worldwide for cardholder + 1 guest; includes DXB, AUH, major hubs.</dd>

  <dt>Skywards status</dt>
  <dd>Silver tier upgrade for the welcome window — priority check-in, extra baggage, tier miles toward Gold. Lost from year two unless you re-qualify on flown tier miles.</dd>

  <dt>Express Miles add-on</dt>
  <dd>Optional AED 250 + 5% VAT/month earns 50% more miles on non-Emirates spend and replaces the annual fee in months it's active.</dd>

  <dt>Salary transfer</dt>
  <dd>Not strictly required at AED 30,000/month, but improves approval odds. ENBD typically asks for 3 months of payslips + a stamped salary certificate — see the <a href="/salary-transfer/">salary-transfer tracker</a>.</dd>

  <dt>Fee waiver mechanics</dt>
  <dd>AED 100,000 is cumulative annual, not per statement. Resets each card anniversary. ENBD applies the waiver retroactively — the fee posts and is reversed, not skipped at the threshold.</dd>
</dl>

## Skywards Infinite vs Skywards Signature

The Infinite makes the most sense if both conditions hold: you'll trigger the
year-one bonus quickly **and** you'll spend AED 100,000+ a year on the card
ongoing. Otherwise the Signature carries lower fee risk for a similar earn

## Watch out for

- **Devaluation risk.** Skywards has repriced the partner award chart twice
  in five years. Keep a redemption goal close at hand rather than banking miles long-term
- **Lounge provider.** Visa Airport Companion, not Priority Pass. Coverage is strong
  in the Gulf, Asia, and most of Europe; double-check less-trafficked regional airports
- **Status match expires.** Silver in year one is generous; you lose it in
  year two unless your flown tier miles re-qualify you
- **Spend-waiver cliff.** Slipping under AED 100,000 in any year reinstates the
  AED 1,575 charge — model your year-two and year-three spend before applying
- **Wrong carrier?** If you fly Etihad more than Emirates, the
  [Etihad Guest Inspire](/cards/emirates-nbd-etihad-guest-inspire/) or
  [FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) carry
  lower fees and similar earn rates on the Abu Dhabi carrier

## Bottom line

For frequent Emirates flyers spending AED 100,000 or more on the card each
year, the Infinite pays back inside year one — but it takes the full
package to do it: the 100,000-mile welcome bonus is worth AED 2,000 at our
[2 fils-per-mile baseline](/airlines/skywards/) — roughly two-thirds of the
AED 3,149 joining fee — and the lounge access and Silver status close the
gap. The spend-waiver clears the AED 1,575 renewal from year two, and
2 miles per USD on Emirates, flydubai, duty free, and food delivery
compounds faster than any other UAE-issued Visa on those categories.

Below AED 100,000 of annual spend the math tightens — the renewal posts in
full each year and the Signature earns enough on the same categories to make
the fee gap hard to justify. A working break-even rule: **AED 100,000+ annual
spend with at least one Y-to-J Emirates upgrade booked from the welcome bonus
inside year one.** Outside that profile, take the Signature.
