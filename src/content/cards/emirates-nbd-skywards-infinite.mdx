## Earn rates

Earn rates are credited as **Skywards Miles per USD 1** of eligible spend (USD 1 ≈ AED 3.67;
2 miles per USD on partner categories works out to roughly 0.54 miles per AED 1):

<EarnRateTable card="emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite" />

Miles post directly to your Emirates Skywards account — no transfer step.

<HotTip>
ENBD's marketing quotes miles per **USD**, not per AED. The 2× partner rate
works out to ~0.54 miles per AED 1 spent — competitive but not as eye-catching
as "2 miles per dollar" reads on the first scan. Compare like-for-like by
converting other cards' AED-quoted rates to USD before benchmarking.
</HotTip>

## Welcome bonus breakdown

<WelcomeBonusCallout card="emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite" />

Headline value sits around AED 2,000 at our [2 fils-per-mile baseline](/airlines/skywards/);
a single Y-to-J upgrade on a regional sector lifts the effective rate higher.

<HotTip label="Hot tip — best use">
The clearest way to break even on year one is a Y-to-J upgrade on a regional sector
(DXB–MAA, DXB–COK, DXB–BLR are usually 30k–50k miles each way at the saver rate).

renewal before any earn-rate spend.
</HotTip>

## Key conditions to know

<dl class="dp-conditions">
  <dt>Lounge access</dt>
  <dd>Visa Airport Companion App, not Priority Pass. Unlimited at 1,000+ lounges worldwide for cardholder + 1 guest; includes DXB, AUH, major hubs.</dd>

  <dt>Skywards status</dt>
  <dd>Silver tier upgrade for the welcome window — priority check-in, extra baggage, tier miles toward Gold. Lost from year two unless you re-qualify on flown tier miles.</dd>

  <dt>Express Miles add-on</dt>
  <dd>Optional AED 250 + 5% VAT/month earns 50% more miles on non-Emirates spend and replaces the annual fee in months it's active.</dd>

  <dt>Salary transfer</dt>
  <dd>Not strictly required at AED 30,000/month, but improves approval odds. ENBD typically asks for 3 months of payslips + a stamped salary certificate — see the <a href="/salary-transfer/">salary-transfer tracker</a>.</dd>

  <dt>Fee waiver mechanics</dt>
  <dd>AED 100,000 is cumulative annual, not per statement. Resets each card anniversary. ENBD applies the waiver retroactively — the fee posts and is reversed, not skipped at the threshold.</dd>
</dl>

## Skywards Infinite vs Skywards Signature

<CardComparison cards={["emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite", "emirates-nbd-skywards-signature"]} />

The Infinite makes the most sense if both conditions hold: you'll trigger the
year-one bonus quickly **and** you'll spend AED 100,000+ a year on the card
ongoing. Otherwise the Signature carries lower fee risk for a similar earn
profile.

## Watch out for

- **Devaluation risk.** Skywards has repriced the partner award chart twice
  in five years. A 2 miles per USD earn on partner categories is only valuable
  if the underlying miles hold their value — keep a redemption goal close at hand
- **Lounge provider.** Visa Airport Companion coverage is strong in the Gulf, Asia,
  and most of Europe; double-check less-trafficked regional airports before you travel
- **Status match expires.** Silver in year one is generous; you lose it in
  year two unless your flown tier miles re-qualify you
- **Spend-waiver cliff.** Slipping under AED 100,000 in any year reinstates the
  AED 1,575 charge — model your year-two and year-three spend before applying
- **Joining-fee hit.** AED 3,148.95 in year one is recovered only if you
  trigger the full welcome bonus and use the miles. If either fails, the
  Signature is the cheaper entry point
- **Better alternatives at lower fees.** If you fly Etihad more than Emirates,
  the [Etihad Guest Inspire](/cards/emirates-nbd-etihad-guest-inspire/) or
  the [FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) carry
  lower fees and similar earn rates on the Abu Dhabi carrier
