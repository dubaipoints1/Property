The FAB Elite Credit Card is pitched at the upper end of FAB's salary-transfer
funnel, bundling a large welcome bonus with an ESG-themed rewards layer. It
requires a salary transfer to FAB and is positioned for residents willing to
move primary banking for the joining offer.

## What you actually get

The welcome bonus is headline up to 500,000 FAB Rewards — the conversion to
AED-equivalent value depends on redemption category and is not published as
a flat rate, so calibrate against your intended redemption channel.

Base earn is 1 FAB Reward per AED 1 of eligible spend, with bonus earn at
"eco-friendly" merchants under a sustainability programme that also funds a
tree-planting initiative tied to spend. Annual fee is AED 2,500 with no
first-year waiver published at time of verification. FX fee is 2.49% — in
line with FAB's premium Visa range. The headline lifestyle perk is a
half-board stay for two adults and two children at a partner resort, redeemed
through the FAB Mobile app.

## Who should open it

The Elite is built for the resident who has already committed to moving salary
to FAB — typically someone in the AED 15,000–30,000 monthly band who values
the welcome haul over week-to-week rewards optimisation. It also suits buyers
who genuinely route discretionary spend through ESG-tagged merchants; for
everyone else, the eco-earn layer is essentially marketing.

## Where it falls short

Salary-transfer lock-in is the headline cost — leaving FAB inside the offer
tenure triggers clawback under FAB's salary-transfer terms, so model the
exit before the entry.

The 1x base earn is uncompetitive against the FAB Cashback's 5x on dining and
shopping, and there is no published annual-fee waiver path.

Worth opening for the welcome bonus if you were going to move salary anyway;
not worth moving salary for.
