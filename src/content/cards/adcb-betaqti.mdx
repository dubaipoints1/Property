The ADCB Betaqti — "my card" in Arabic — is ADCB's ADCB TouchPoints-currency
Visa positioned toward the UAE national family-spend profile. It earns ADCB
TouchPoints redeemable in-store across thousands of UAE merchants, with an
optional exchange into Emirates Skywards Miles, and an anniversary points
bonus that scales with annual card spend.

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="adcb-betaqti" />

There is a conflict on the annual fee. L2 records the annual fee at **AED 0**,
but the ADCB product page free-text references an **AED 2,100** annual fee
inclusive of VAT. We have surfaced both values rather than picking one — verify
against the current ADCB Schedule of Fees before applying. The FX fee is
**0.525%**, materially below the 2.49% norm on premium Visa Infinites — a
genuine strength of the ADCB TouchPoints card family for overseas spend.

## Watch out for

- **Salary-transfer commitment.** Required at the AED 10,000 salary bar. Worth
  weighing against the salary-transfer reward ADCB is currently running — see
  the [salary-transfer tracker](/salary-transfer/).
- **Annual-fee conflict.** L2 records AED 0; the issuer product page free-text
  references AED 2,100 inclusive of VAT. Verify against the current ADCB
  Schedule before applying.
- **The "up to 3 TouchPoints" headline.** The qualifying-spend conditions for
  the higher earn rate are not in L2 — the base rate is 1 TouchPoint per AED 1.
- **TouchPoints exchange ratio.** ADCB TouchPoints exchange into Emirates
  Skywards Miles per the perks list, but the conversion rate is not published
  in L2. Without it, the airline-route value cannot be computed.
- **Positioned for the UAE national profile.** Branding and merchant network
  are weighted to local family spend; expat readers may find broader-utility
  cashback cards at the same salary bar more useful.

## Is it worth it?

For a UAE national who will run household spend through the card, redeem
ADCB TouchPoints at LuLu, Carrefour, ADNOC and the broader in-store partner
network, and value the anniversary points scaling on sustained card spend: the
Betaqti is a coherent product. The 0.525% FX fee is a genuine strength if any
of that spend is overseas, and the Emirates Skywards Miles exchange route
gives the option of routing local card spend into the airline programme
indirectly — once the conversion ratio is confirmed.

For an expat reader, the maths inverts. The
[Emirates NBD Skywards Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite/) or
the [FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) earn
directly into the airline programme without the ADCB TouchPoints intermediate
step and without the salary-transfer requirement. The Betaqti's anniversary
mechanic is a discipline reward; the co-brands are a redemption play. Match
the card to which mechanic you actually use.

## Bottom line

A UAE-national-leaning ADCB TouchPoints card with a 750,000-anniversary-point
headline, an in-store redemption network across thousands of UAE merchants,
and an Emirates Skywards Miles exchange route on top. **Apply** if you're a
UAE national who will run household spend through the card and redeem locally.
**Skip** if you're an expat fly-Emirates-or-Etihad reader — a direct-earn
co-brand earns more per dirham and without the salary-transfer commitment.
