## Fee summary

  <dd>Talabat credit sits in your Talabat Pay balance and is redeemed at checkout against any Talabat order — food, Talabat Mart, grocery, pharmacy. No transfers to airlines, hotels, statement credit, AED cash-out or third-party programmes.</dd>

  <dt>Talabat Pro membership</dt>
  <dd>Free delivery on Talabat food orders of AED 50 and above, and Talabat Mart, grocery and pharmacy orders of AED 100 and above from stores tagged 'pro'.</dd>

## Watch out for

- **Captive-currency payout.** Talabat credit only redeems inside the Talabat app.
  If you stop ordering there, the balance traps the rewards.
- **Existing-customer welcome is much weaker.** AED 200 for existing ADCB cardholders
  versus AED 750 new-to-bank — this card is sharpest as a first-ADCB-card.
- **Earn rate clarity.** The L2 schedule we hold lists only the base 1 Talabat credit
  per AED 1; the accelerated Talabat-app rate is flagged for fact-checker review
  against the live product page.
- **Off-Talabat earn is modest.** Pair this with a flat-rate cashback card for spend
  that doesn't go through Talabat.

## Is it worth it?

For a Talabat-heavy household new to ADCB, the welcome credit plus unlimited free
delivery on qualifying orders carries the card on its own — AED 750 of opening
value plus a recurring delivery-fee saving that adds up if you order weekly.

It is the wrong card for an occasional Talabat user, or for an existing ADCB
cardholder for whom the welcome collapses to AED 200. In either case a flat-rate
AED-denominated cashback product is the cleaner hold.

## Bottom line

A free-for-life food-delivery co-brand whose maths is sharp for new-to-bank
Talabat regulars and weak for everyone else. **Apply** if you fit the new-to-bank
criteria and order on Talabat most weeks. **Skip** if you already hold an ADCB
card or use Talabat only occasionally.
