The Emirates NBD dnata World Credit Card is the premium tier in ENBD's
dnata co-brand pair, co-branded with dnata Travel, the Emirates Group's
UAE travel agency. It earns **dnata Points** redeemable directly against
bookings made through dnata Travel — flights, hotels, holidays — and is
positioned for households that route the bulk of their travel spend
through the agency anyway.

<HotTip>
The single feature that distinguishes the dnata World from the dnata
Platinum is the **0% FX fee**. A household running AED 30,000 of
foreign-currency travel a year — hotels priced in USD or EUR, airfare on
non-AED tickets, ground services abroad — saves roughly AED 600 a year
against a 1.99% card on the FX line alone, before any dnata Points earn.
Outside foreign-currency spend, the Platinum's 10x travel and 5x
shopping multipliers earn more per dirham than the World's flat 1.5%
baseline.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-dnata-world" />

The fee structure is heavier than the headline suggests: **AED 1,048.95**
joining fee in year one, **AED 1,048.95** annual fee from year two, both
VAT-inclusive. Year-one running cost is north of AED 2,000 before any
spend. **FX is 0%** — the structural advantage. Minimum salary is
**AED 20,000 a month** and **salary transfer to Emirates NBD is
required**, which adds operational friction if you later want to move
banks.

## Key conditions to know

<dl class="dp-conditions">
  <dt>Welcome bonus mechanics</dt>
  <dd>Up to 2,500 dnata Points: 1,000 on payment of the joining fee, plus 1,500 on AED 25,000 of retail spend across the first three billing statements. Modest in absolute terms — the welcome alone does not cover the AED 1,048.95 year-one fee at any plausible dnata Point valuation.</dd>

  <dt>Category earn</dt>
  <dd>15% at Costa Coffee, City Sightseeing, Giraffe and dnata Travel; 10% at MMI, Al Hamra Cellar, Arabian Adventures, Le Clos and on Duty Free shopping; 1.5% on all other retail spend. There are no general-category multipliers on groceries, fuel or dining.</dd>

  <dt>FX fee</dt>
  <dd>0% foreign-currency fee — the only ENBD travel card with a true zero-FX line. Material on USD, EUR and GBP hotel and airfare bookings.</dd>

  <dt>Redemption mechanics</dt>
  <dd>dnata Points redeem against bookings made through dnata Travel — branches, dnata.travel and the call centre — at the agency's published conversion. Points do not transfer to airline or hotel programmes; this is a closed-loop currency.</dd>

  <dt>Lounge access</dt>
  <dd>Complimentary access to 1,000+ airport lounges worldwide via the Mastercard Travel Pass programme. Verify current visit cap and guesting policy on the issuer page before relying on it for a specific trip.</dd>

  <dt>Lifestyle perks</dt>
  <dd>Concierge service, complimentary access to top UAE golf courses, and valet parking at selected Abu Dhabi locations. Useful adjuncts but not the reason to hold the card.</dd>

  <dt>Eligibility</dt>
  <dd>Minimum salary AED 20,000/month; salary transfer to Emirates NBD required; UAE residency required; salaried employment.</dd>

## Watch out for

- **Year-one cost is high.** AED 1,048.95 joining + AED 1,048.95 annual
  = roughly AED 2,098 before any spend. The 2,500-point welcome and the
  0% FX edge need to earn that back before the card pays for itself.
- **Flat 1.5% base earn.** Outside the partner list, there are no
  category multipliers — groceries, fuel and dining all pay 1.5%, which
  is uncompetitive against a Plus-Points-earning ENBD Duo (5% on
  groceries, fuel, utilities, education) or a flat-rate cashback card.
- **Closed-loop redemption.** dnata Points are useful only inside
  dnata's ecosystem. If your travel pattern shifts to direct airline
  bookings, hotel co-brands or international OTAs, the loyalty value
  evaporates.
- **Salary-transfer lock-in.** Moving salary out of Emirates NBD inside
  the offer tenure can trigger clawback; the [salary-transfer
  tracker](/salary-transfer/) lists current switching offers worth
  stacking against this application if you're moving anyway.

## Is it worth it?

For a UAE household running AED 30,000-plus of travel a year through
dnata Travel, earning AED 20,000-plus a month, and willing to move
salary to Emirates NBD: the 0% FX fee is the single reason to hold this
card. On a family that pays USD-priced hotels and EUR-priced rail across
European summer travel, the FX saving alone clears the annual fee, and
the partner-brand earn on Costa, MMI, Arabian Adventures and Duty Free
is genuinely useful on top.

For a reader whose travel runs direct with Emirates, Etihad or Qatar —
or who books through international OTAs — this is the wrong loyalty
currency. An [ENBD Skywards Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite/)
or [FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) earns
portable airline miles instead, and the [UAE transfer ratios
pillar](/guides/uae-transfer-ratios-2026/) covers how bank-points routes
compare against direct airline co-brands.

## Bottom line

A narrow-purpose premium travel card whose single structural advantage
is the 0% FX fee. **Apply** if dnata Travel is your default booking
channel, your salary clears AED 20,000, and you're willing to move
salary to Emirates NBD. **Skip** if you book direct with airlines, want
portable miles, or are not committing to a salary transfer.

The [Emirates NBD dnata Platinum](/cards/emirates-nbd-dnata-platinum/)
is the right entry into the dnata pair at the AED 5,000 salary bar
without salary-transfer lock-in — read it alongside this review before
deciding which tier matches your spend pattern.
