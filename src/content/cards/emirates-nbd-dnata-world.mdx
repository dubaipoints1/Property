The Emirates NBD dnata World Credit Card is co-branded with dnata Travel,
the UAE's largest travel-services group. It earns dnata Travel Points
redeemable against bookings made through dnata's channels (flights, hotels,
holidays). It requires salary transfer and is positioned as a mid-tier
travel companion product.

## What you actually get

Earn is 1 dnata Travel Point per AED 1 spent — a flat structure with no
category bonuses. Annual fee is AED 1,048. FX fee is 0%, which is the
single most useful feature for international bookings. Salary transfer is
required, with a minimum monthly salary of AED 20,000. Headline perk on
the World Mastercard tier is the dnata loyalty programme integration —
points post automatically and redeem at a published conversion against
dnata bookings.

## Who should open it

The card matches a household that books at least AED 30,000–50,000 of travel
a year through dnata's website or branches, earns AED 20,000+ a month, and
is comfortable moving salary to Emirates NBD. The most common rational holder
is a dual-income family that takes two long-haul holidays a year and an
annual GCC break, all booked through dnata.

## Where it falls short

The 1x flat earn is unremarkable against a 2.5x Skywards card or a 4x
groceries Plus Points card; if you do not book the bulk of your travel
through dnata, the loyalty currency is largely stranded. Salary-transfer
clawback applies if you exit Emirates NBD inside the offer tenure.

A useful card if dnata is already your travel agent; not a reason to make
dnata your travel agent.
