The Emirates NBD Visa Platinum is the bank's no-fee mid-tier card, positioned
for salaried residents in the AED 12,000–30,000 monthly band who want a clean
everyday rewards earner without committing to a salary transfer or paying an
annual fee.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 4 Emirates NBD Plus Points per AED 1 on groceries, 2 per AED 1
on fuel, and 1 per AED 1 on everything else — structurally identical to the
ENBD Visa Infinite. Annual fee is zero. FX fee is 0%, which on a no-fee card
is genuinely unusual and makes this a strong companion card for international
online spend (Amazon US, foreign airfares booked in USD, etc.). Salary
transfer is not required. Standard Visa Platinum benefits — purchase
protection, basic travel insurance — apply at the network level.

## Who should open it

The card matches a salaried resident earning AED 12,000–30,000 a month who
already has at least one ENBD relationship (account, mortgage, or other card).
It works particularly well as a secondary card for cardholders whose primary
card lives elsewhere — use the Visa Platinum for groceries, fuel and
foreign-currency online spend, and let your primary card handle dining and
travel.

## Where it falls short

Plus Points redeem at materially less value per point than direct airline
miles, so do not treat this as a travel card — convert points to statement
credit or shopping vouchers and accept the modest implied return. Without
salary transfer, ENBD's underwriting weights existing-customer history
heavily; newcomers should expect to be offered the Visa Flexi instead.

The strongest no-fee Visa from a UAE flagship bank, full stop.
