<HotTip>
On a 7.5% headline, the AED 2,625 joining fee alone breaks even at roughly **AED 35,000 a
month** of qualifying Emaar spend in year one (AED 2,625 ÷ 7.5% ÷ 12). Add the AED 1,575
renewal and the year-one break-even climbs higher still. Run your own twelve-month Emaar
total before applying — anything below that line, and a no-fee ENBD Plus Points card plus
a separate U by Emaar account earns the loyalty side without the AED 4,200 of card fees.

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-u-by-emaar-infinite" />

The card carries an **AED 2,625 joining fee** in year one, on top of the **AED 1,575
annual renewal** from year two onwards — both inclusive of VAT. There is **no published
spend-based waiver** on either fee. The **foreign-currency fee is 1.99%**, levied on every
non-AED transaction. As with any rewards card, clear the statement in full each month —
the 3.25% monthly interest charge will erase any UPoints value many times over.

## Watch out for

- **AED 35,000/month of Emaar spend is the break-even.** Below that, the AED 2,625 joining
  fee is not earned back on the 7.5% rate in year one. Run a twelve-month Emaar audit
  before applying — restaurants, mall purchases, hotel stays, cinema tickets — and confirm
  the number stands up.
- **UPoints lock you into the Emaar ecosystem.** They redeem at Emaar's 2,500+ partner
  outlets and nowhere else — no airline transfers, no hotel-programme conversion. If your
  travel ambitions sit outside Emaar's footprint, a Skywards or hotel-co-brand card will
  carry more redemption optionality.
- **The 1.5% on non-Emaar spend is thin.** It is only marginally above the ENBD Plus
  Points base; the SC Platinum X or Mashreq Cashback earns more on the same non-Emaar
  spend. Treat this as a single-purpose Emaar-spend card, not an everyday earner.
- **The Reel Cinemas BOGO gate.** The benefit is conditional on AED 5,000 minimum card
  spend in the month. Check your typical monthly card spend before relying on it.
- **Valet redirection at Dubai Mall.** Grand Drive valet has been periodically rerouted
  during Dubai Mall expansion works; access is via an OTP generated in the U by Emaar app.
  Re-verify the current valet location before relying on it for a planned visit.

## Is it worth it?

For the household that genuinely spends AED 35,000+ a month inside the Emaar footprint —
Dubai Mall regulars, Address loyalists, families that route their dining and weekend
entertainment through the Emaar list — the 7.5% UPoints rate is unmatched in the UAE
market and the AED 4,200 of year-one fees is recoverable. Layer on automatic U by Emaar
Platinum status, suite upgrades and the Visa Infinite chrome, and the proposition stands
up for the right reader.

It is the wrong card for everyone else. Non-Emaar spend earns 1.5% — only marginally above
the ENBD Plus Points base — and UPoints lock you into Emaar's ecosystem with no airline or
hotel-programme escape valve. Below AED 35,000 a month of qualifying spend the AED 2,625
joining fee does not pencil out, and a no-fee ENBD Visa Flexi plus a free-standing U by
Emaar account captures the loyalty side without the card fees.

## Bottom line

A bullseye for Dubai Mall locals and Emaar-hotel loyalists; an overpaid Visa Infinite for
everyone else. **Apply** if your year of Emaar spend clears AED 420,000 and you value the
Platinum status. **Skip** if most of your spend lands outside the Emaar footprint — the
1.5% non-Emaar rate and the AED 4,200 year-one fees rule it out as a default card.
