The Emirates NBD Visa Infinite is the bank's flagship Plus-Points-earning card
for the AED 30,000+ salary segment. It sits at the top of the conventional
(non-co-branded) Visa range and is positioned as the all-rounder for ENBD's
priority-segment customers.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 4 Emirates NBD Plus Points per AED 1 on groceries, 2 per AED 1
on fuel, and 1 per AED 1 on everything else, redeemable inside the ENBD
SmartPASS / Plus Points portal. The headline structural benefit is a 0% FX
fee — almost unique in the UAE Visa Infinite tier, and worth several hundred
dirhams a year for anyone who spends in USD or GBP online. Annual fee is
AED 1,575. Salary transfer to Emirates NBD is required, with a minimum
monthly salary of AED 30,000. Standard Visa Infinite benefits — airport
lounge access, travel insurance, concierge — are included; programme detail
should be checked against the latest issuer summary before relying on it.

## Who should open it

The card matches a household income above AED 30,000 a month where moving
salary to Emirates NBD is on the table — typically professionals 5+ years
into UAE residency, with AECB history in place and meaningful grocery and
online-international spend. It pairs well with a single co-brand card for
travel-loyalty earning; on its own, Plus Points are an underwhelming
redemption currency.

## Where it falls short

Plus Points conversion ratios remain weak versus direct airline-mile earning,
so a Skywards Infinite holder will get more redemption value per AED spent on
travel. The salary-transfer requirement carries clawback exposure — review
the bank's salary-transfer T&Cs before signing if there's any chance you'll
change jobs or leave the UAE inside the 12-month tenure.

A sound default card for ENBD's priority customers; not a destination card on
its own merits.
