## Key conditions to know

<dl class="dp-conditions">
  <dt>Earn structure</dt>
  <dd>Up to 1.5 TouchPoints per AED 1 on general spend; 2 TouchPoints per AED 1 on fuel. ADCB does not publish a spend ceiling on the 1.5 rate at the product-page level (flagged).</dd>

  <dt>Monthly bonus</dt>
  <dd>15,000 bonus TouchPoints credited when monthly card spend reaches AED 15,000 — a clean linear trigger, no category restriction. Roughly doubles the marginal earn at exactly that spend level.</dd>

  <dt>Lounge access</dt>
  <dd>Visa Infinite lounge access via the Visa Airport Companion app; cardholder + 1 guest at 1,000+ lounges worldwide. Not Priority Pass.</dd>

  <dt>Redemption pathways</dt>
  <dd>Instant redemption at thousands of UAE merchants (utilities, Salik, retail) at ADCB's published merchant rate; or conversion to Emirates Skywards Miles (conversion ratio not published on this card's page).</dd>

## Watch out for

- **Direct Skywards may beat TouchPoints for Emirates loyalists.** ENBD's Skywards
  Infinite earns 2 Skywards Miles per USD (~0.54 miles per AED 1) on Emirates,
  flydubai, duty free and online food delivery. If you fly Emirates twice a year
  or more, model both cards on your real spend before applying — TouchPoints
  conversion typically lags direct co-brand earning.
- **Conversion ratio not published.** ADCB doesn't state the TouchPoints-to-Skywards
  conversion on the product page. If airline transfers are central to your plan,
  confirm the current ratio with ADCB before applying.
- **Rate cap unclear.** "Up to 1.5 TouchPoints per AED 1" implies a ceiling that
  isn't published. High monthly spenders should verify the post-cap rate.
- **Year-two fee unspecified.** "Prevailing rate" without a figure on the product
  page. Confirm at application.
- **Talabat perk has a spend gate.** The 20% off Talabat orders requires AED 5,000
  of monthly card spend — easy to clear at this salary tier, but a hidden condition.
- **Devaluation risk sits with ADCB.** TouchPoints redemption values can be re-rated
  without notice. Direct co-brand earning shifts that risk to the airline programme
  instead.

## Is it worth it?

For an executive earning AED 30,000+ who keeps banking relationships diversified —
doesn't want a salary-transfer lock-in, but still wants lounge access and an
accelerated rewards rate on a Visa Infinite — the TouchPoints Infinite is a credible
flagship. The 1.5 TouchPoints per AED headline rate, the 15,000-point monthly bonus
at AED 15,000 of spend, and the lounge-and-lifestyle stack add up to a defensible
premium Visa Infinite proposition. At a moderate AED 20,000 of monthly spend you'd
earn 30,000 base points plus the 15,000 monthly bonus — 45,000 TouchPoints a month,
540,000 a year before any anniversary bonuses.

It's the wrong card if your travel is concentrated on Emirates — ENBD's Skywards
Infinite earns direct miles at 2 per USD on Emirates spend, beating the TouchPoints
conversion math on like-for-like flying. It's also the wrong card if you specifically
want Priority Pass (Visa Airport Companion is a different programme with different
lounge coverage) or if conversion-ratio transparency is non-negotiable.

## Bottom line

ADCB's flagship Visa Infinite for the AED 30,000+ executive who values flexible
redemption and lounge access without committing to a single airline. **Apply** if
you want a premium Visa Infinite without a salary-transfer lock-in and value
TouchPoints' merchant-redemption flexibility. **Skip** if you fly Emirates almost
exclusively — ENBD's Skywards Infinite earns direct miles at a comparable fee
profile.
