<HotTip>
The 1.5 TouchPoints per AED 1 headline applies to **general (uncategorised) spend
only**. The category breakdown clips back hard: 0.4 per AED at supermarkets, auto
dealers, insurance and quick-service restaurants; 0.2 per AED at fuel, telecom,
utilities, education, government, real estate, charity and transport. Model your
actual spend against the category split before assuming the 1.5 rate. The
15,000-point monthly bonus at AED 15,000 spend is the cleanest uplift.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="adcb-touchpoints-infinite" />

There is no annual fee in year one. From year two the product page applies ADCB's
"prevailing rate" and does not split out a separate figure at the card-page level —
request it in writing at application. Foreign-currency transactions carry a
**0.525%** Visa margin, well below the typical UAE band.

## Watch out for

- **Direct Skywards may beat TouchPoints for Emirates loyalists.** ENBD's Skywards
  Infinite earns 2 Skywards Miles per USD (~0.54 miles per AED 1) on Emirates,
  flydubai, duty free and online food delivery. If you fly Emirates twice a year
  or more, model both cards on your real spend before applying — TouchPoints
  conversion typically lags direct co-brand earning.
- **Conversion ratio not published.** ADCB does not state the TouchPoints-to-Skywards
  conversion at the card-page level. If airline transfers are central to your plan,
  get the current ratio in writing from ADCB before applying.
- **Category clip-back matters.** The 1.5 headline applies to general (uncategorised)
  spend only — supermarkets, fuel, telecom and utilities earn a fraction of that.
  Model your real spend against the category split before assuming the headline.
- **Year-two fee at "prevailing rate".** ADCB does not split the year-two figure out
  on the product page. Request it in writing at application and screenshot the term sheet.
- **Talabat perk has a spend gate.** The 20% off Talabat orders requires AED 5,000
  of monthly card spend — easy to clear at this salary tier, but a hidden condition.
- **Devaluation risk sits with ADCB.** TouchPoints redemption values can be re-rated
  without notice. Direct co-brand earning shifts that risk to the airline programme
  instead.

## Is it worth it?

For an executive earning AED 30,000+ who keeps banking relationships diversified —
doesn't want a salary-transfer lock-in, but still wants lounge access and an
accelerated rewards rate on a Visa Infinite — the TouchPoints Infinite is a credible
flagship. The 1.5 TouchPoints per AED rate on general spend, the 15,000-point monthly
bonus at AED 15,000 of spend, and the lounge-and-lifestyle stack add up to a
defensible premium Visa Infinite proposition. At AED 20,000 of monthly spend
weighted toward general (uncategorised) merchants — restaurants, travel,
discretionary retail — you'd earn 30,000 base points plus the 15,000 monthly bonus.
If that same AED 20,000 sits on supermarkets, fuel and utilities, the maths is
materially worse: the category clip-back means the headline doesn't apply.

It's the wrong card if your travel is concentrated on Emirates — ENBD's Skywards
Infinite earns direct miles at 2 per USD on Emirates spend, beating the TouchPoints
conversion math on like-for-like flying. It's also the wrong card if you specifically
want Priority Pass (Visa Airport Companion is a different programme with different
lounge coverage) or if conversion-ratio transparency is non-negotiable.

## Bottom line

ADCB's flagship Visa Infinite for the AED 30,000+ executive who values flexible
redemption and lounge access without committing to a single airline. **Apply** if
you want a premium Visa Infinite without a salary-transfer lock-in and value
TouchPoints' merchant-redemption flexibility. **Skip** if you fly Emirates almost
exclusively — ENBD's Skywards Infinite earns direct miles at a comparable fee
profile.
