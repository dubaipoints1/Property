The ADCB 365 Cashback Credit Card is the bank's flagship everyday cashback
Visa — built around accelerated rates on dining, online, groceries and fuel,
with a hard AED 5,000 monthly-spend floor and an AED 1,000 monthly cashback
ceiling that bracket who the card is actually for.

## Earn rates

Cashback is paid as a percentage of spend per category. The 1% on
"everything else" also applies to **all international retail purchases**,
regardless of which domestic category the merchant would normally fall into:

<HotTip>
The 365 Cashback only earns once you cross **AED 5,000 of spend in a calendar
month**. Below that floor, the entire month's spend earns nothing. The
cashback is then capped at **AED 1,000 a month**, which the strongest
categories hit at roughly AED 16,700 of dining or online spend alone. The
card is therefore at its best between AED 5,000 and around AED 20,000 of
monthly spend on the listed categories.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="adcb-365-cashback" />

There is no annual fee in year one. From year two the primary card carries
**AED 383.25 (incl. VAT)**, with no spend-based waiver. Foreign-currency
transactions carry a **2.99%** margin, in line with the typical UAE market
band — no FX advantage on this card, take a dedicated travel card abroad.

## Watch out for

- **The AED 5,000 spend floor.** A holiday month, a maternity break, a
  freelance dry patch — any month under AED 5,000 of card spend earns AED 0
  cashback. Plan accordingly.
- **The AED 1,000 cashback ceiling.** The 6% headline tops out at around
  AED 16,700 of dining/online spend alone. Heavier monthly spenders should
  pair this card with a flat-rate card for spend above the cap.
- **International spend earns 1% only.** This is a domestic card. Take a
  travel card abroad — the 6% category rates do not follow you across borders.
- **No spend-based waiver on the year-two fee.** Budget for the
  AED 383.25 from year two — unlike most ADCB cards we cover, this one
  has no waiver mechanic.
- **Clawback realities.** Welcome bonuses are typically reversible if the
  card is cancelled within 12 months — confirm with ADCB before applying if
  you plan to close the card early.

## Is it worth it?

For a UAE resident whose monthly card spend reliably clears AED 5,000 and is
weighted toward dining, online and groceries, the 365 Cashback earns its
place. Five months at the AED 1,000 ceiling clears AED 5,000 of cashback
against an AED 383.25 year-two fee — comfortable headroom — and the
AED 365 welcome bonus covers year two on its own if triggered.

It is the wrong card for anyone whose monthly spend swings below AED 5,000,
because the entire month's earn falls to zero on those months. For that
profile, ADCB's Essential Cashback sibling has a lower AED 1,000 monthly
floor and is the safer pick. For international travel spend the rate is a
flat 1% — take a dedicated travel card abroad.

## Bottom line

A strong category-cashback Visa for consistent mid-to-high spenders on
dining, online and groceries. **Apply** if you'll reliably clear AED 5,000
of monthly spend on the named categories. **Skip** if your month-to-month
spend swings below the floor — every quiet month wipes that month's earn.
