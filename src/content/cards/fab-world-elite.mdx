The FAB World Elite Mastercard is FAB's top-end travel-and-lifestyle card,
positioned around lounge access, premium insurance and a heavy international
earn multiplier. There is no published minimum salary at the time of
verification, but residency is required and the underwriting tilts to higher
incomes.

## What you actually get

Earn is 10 FAB Rewards per AED 1 on international (foreign currency) spend
and 1 FAB Reward per AED 1 on everything else. The card carries unlimited
worldwide lounge access for the primary cardholder and a guest — the most
useful perk in the package — plus a 24/7 concierge service and travel
insurance coverage of up to USD 500,000 (~AED 1,836,000). Annual fee is AED
2,500 with no first-year waiver published. FX fee is 2.49%, in line with
FAB's premium tier.

## Who should open it

The card matches a traveller-resident profile: AED 25,000+ household income,
three or more international trips a year, regular online spend in USD or GBP,
and someone who values lounge access enough to budget for it. It pairs well
as the international companion to a domestic cashback card — keep the World
Elite for foreign-currency and travel spend, run AED-denominated daily
spending through a 5%-category cashback product.

## Where it falls short

The 1x AED-spend floor is the structural weakness; a resident whose travel
patterns slow down loses most of the card's value while still paying AED 2,500
a year. The 2.49% FX fee shaves around a quarter off the headline 10x
international earn once converted to AED-value-per-dirham-spent. And without
a first-year fee waiver, you commit AED 2,500 before you have run a single
qualifying transaction.

A genuine premium-travel card for the right user; an expensive 1x earner for
the wrong one.
