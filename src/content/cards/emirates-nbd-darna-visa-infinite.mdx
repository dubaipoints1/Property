and 10% back as Darna Points at participating Emaar partners — redeemable against rent,
service charges, Emaar F&B and Reel Cinemas at the providers inside the network — plus
automatic Darna Platinum tier and the Visa Infinite benefit suite.

<HotTip>
On a 10% headline, the AED 1,575 annual fee breaks even at roughly **AED 15,000 a month** of
qualifying Emaar spend (AED 1,575 ÷ 10% ÷ 12). Spend less than that inside the partner list
and the fee is not recovered on category earn alone — you are relying on the Platinum tier
and Visa Infinite chrome to make up the gap. Run a twelve-month Emaar audit before applying:
restaurants, mall purchases, hotel stays, cinema. Anything below the line, and a no-fee ENBD
Plus Points or [Darna Select](/cards/emirates-nbd-darna-select-visa/) earns the loyalty side
without the AED 1,575.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-darna-visa-infinite" />

The card carries an **AED 1,575 annual fee inclusive of VAT** with **no published waiver
threshold** — there is no spend-based escape from the fee. Salary transfer to Emirates NBD
is **mandatory** for approval. The **foreign-currency fee is 1.99%** — older marketing
summaries that described this as a 0% FX card are wrong against the current Schedule of
Charges. As with any rewards card, clear the statement in full each month — the standard
ENBD revolving rate will erase any Darna value many times over.

## Watch out for

- **AED 15,000/month of Emaar spend is the break-even on category earn alone.** Below
  that, the AED 1,575 fee is not recovered on the 10% rate; you are paying the difference
  in exchange for Platinum tier and Visa Infinite chrome. Confirm a twelve-month Emaar
  total before applying.
- **Salary-transfer lock-in and clawback.** ENBD requires salary transfer to the bank for
  approval and applies a standard clawback if the account is closed inside the first
  twelve months — the welcome offer and tier status are recovered, and the AED 1,575 fee
  is not refunded. If you may leave the UAE or switch banks within the year, hold off.
- **No airline or hotel transfers on Darna Points.** They redeem at Emaar's partner
  network and nowhere else. There is no points-to-Skywards or points-to-Bonvoy escape
  valve — hold this for genuine Emaar spend, not as an open-ended rewards card.
- **1.99% FX, not 0%.** Legacy marketing summaries describing this as a zero-FX card are
  out of date against the current Schedule of Charges. Not the right card for material
  international spend.
- **Better Infinite alternatives if you are not an Emaar household.** The 1.5% on
  non-partner spend is thin for the Visa Infinite tier. The
  [Skywards Visa Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite/) or the
  [SHARE Visa Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-share-visa-infinite/) earn more per dirham on
  general spend at the same fee band.

## Is it worth it?

For the household whose discretionary spend genuinely lives inside Emaar's footprint at AED
15,000+ a month — Dubai Mall regulars, Address / Vida / Rove loyalists, families that route
dining and weekend entertainment through the Emaar list — the 10% Darna Points rate is the
strongest in the Darna stack, the welcome bonus is meaningful, and the Platinum tier plus
Visa Infinite chrome stack on top. The AED 1,575 fee earns its keep.

It is the wrong card for everyone else. Non-Emaar spend earns 1.5% — thin for the Visa
Infinite tier — Darna Points lock into the Emaar ecosystem, the salary-transfer requirement
adds friction, and the FX fee rules it out as a travel-spend card. Below AED 15,000 a month
of qualifying Emaar spend the fee does not pencil out, and a no-fee Darna Select plus a
broad cashback card captures the loyalty side without the AED 1,575.

## Bottom line

A genuine top-tier earner inside Emaar's footprint; an overpaid Visa Infinite for everyone
else. **Apply** if your year of Emaar spend clears AED 180,000 and you accept the
salary-transfer commitment. **Skip** if most spend lands outside the Emaar list or you may
switch banks within the year — the 1.5% on non-partner spend and the salary-transfer lock
rule it out as a default card.
