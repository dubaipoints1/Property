The Emirates NBD Darna Visa Signature is the mid-tier rung of ENBD's Darna co-brand. AED 315
inclusive of VAT on paper, currently waived for life under a May 2026 promotional offer; AED
12,000 minimum salary, no salary-transfer requirement; 7.5% back as Darna Points at
participating Emaar partners and 1% elsewhere — redeemable against rent, service charges,
Emaar F&B and Reel Cinemas at the providers inside the network.

<HotTip>
The 7.5% partner rate is the upgrade over the [Darna
Select](/cards/emirates-nbd-darna-select-visa/)'s 6.25%, but Darna Points have a single
redemption universe: the Emaar / Darna partner network. They are not transferable to
Skywards, Etihad Guest, Marriott Bonvoy or any cash-equivalent. If your landlord, building
manager or weekly dining list does not sit inside that network, the effective rate on real
spend is the 1% off-network line — which a no-fee flat-rate cashback card will match or
beat. Run a twelve-week spend audit before applying.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-darna-visa-signature" />

The standard fee is **AED 315 inclusive of VAT**, **currently waived for life** under a May
2026 promotional offer from Emirates NBD. Treat the waiver as conditional — confirm at
application that the offer still applies to your slot, and check the renewal letter each
year. The cost to watch is the **1.99% foreign-currency fee** — the legacy "0% FX" line you
may see on older summaries is not the current published figure. As with any rewards card,
clear the statement in full each month — the standard ENBD revolving rate will erase any
Darna value many times over.

## Watch out for

- **The fee waiver is promotional, not contractual.** AED 315 inclusive of VAT is the
  Schedule-of-Charges fee; the lifetime waiver is a May 2026 offer that Emirates NBD can
  end. Plan for the AED 315 returning at renewal — and decide whether the maths still
  pencils at that point.
- **Confirm your Darna partner exposure before applying.** The 7.5% rate only pays at
  outlets inside the Emaar / Darna network. If your rent goes to a non-Emaar landlord and
  your grocery and fuel spend is at non-partner merchants, you are earning 1% on the bulk
  of your card spend.
- **No airline or hotel transfers.** Darna Points have one home: the Emaar / Darna partner
  network. There is no points-to-Skywards or points-to-Marriott escape valve.
- **1.99% FX fee.** Older marketing called this a zero-FX card. The current published
  figure is 1.99%, so it is not the right card for international spend.
- **Clawback on closure.** Standard ENBD card terms claw back welcome offers and tier
  status if the account is closed inside the first twelve months — relevant if you may
  leave the UAE or switch banks within the year.

## Bottom line

For the Emaar-adjacent household at AED 12,000+ a month — tenants in Emaar buildings,
Dubai Mall regulars, households that route dining and weekend entertainment through the
Emaar list — the Darna Signature is a credible step up from the Select while the lifetime
fee waiver holds. The 7.5% partner rate is real money, the AED 350 voucher and 3,000-point
welcome are useful, and Darna Gold tier adds a discount layer inside the network.

It's the wrong card if your spend doesn't live inside the Darna list — 1% non-partner
earn is uncompetitive against flat-rate cashback and the 1.99% FX rules it out abroad.
**Apply** if your spend genuinely lives at Darna partners; **skip** otherwise. If
qualifying Emaar spend exceeds AED 30,000 a month, the
[Darna Visa Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-darna-visa-infinite/)'s 10% partner rate may
justify its AED 1,575 fee — the Signature is the rational middle.
