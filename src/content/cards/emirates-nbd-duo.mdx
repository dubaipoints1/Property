The Emirates NBD Duo is a paired-card product — a Visa plus a complementary
Mastercard or Diners Club on a single credit limit. AED 0 annual fee, 0% FX
fee, no welcome bonus, and a flat 1% Plus Points earn rate.

## Earn rates

| Spend category | Plus Points per AED 1 |
|---|---|
| Everything else | **1** |

Plus Points redeem for Skywards Miles, cashback, or merchant credit at ENBD's
published rates — typically ~1¢ per point at the cashback ceiling.

## Fee summary

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Annual fee | AED 0 |
| FX fee | 0% |
| Minimum monthly salary | AED 12,000 |
| Salary transfer | Not required |

The fee profile is the headline draw: zero ongoing cost, zero FX surcharge
on international transactions.

## Key conditions to know

**Paired-card mechanics:**
- The product issues **two physical cards** on a single credit limit — a Visa
  and a complementary Mastercard or Diners
- The two cards share statement, limit, and minimum payment
- Useful for redundancy (one card declined, use the other) and for splitting
  by partner without managing separate accounts

**Network acceptance:**
- Visa runs everywhere; the paired Mastercard / Diners catches the edge
  cases where one network is preferred or required

## Best for

Households on AED 12,000+ a month who want a primary plus a backup card on
a single account, and who care more about availability and zero FX cost than
about earn rate. Particularly useful for travellers who want network
redundancy abroad.

## Watch out for

- **1% earn is flat and uncompetitive** — outside the FX-fee saving, this
  card returns very little in points
- **No welcome bonus** published on the issuer page at our verification —
  there's no signup runway to defray the (already zero) annual cost
- **Plus Points realise unevenly.** Cashback at par is usually the cleanest
  redemption; the Skywards conversion rate is unfavourable
- **Two cards, one limit.** Spending on both cards depletes the shared
  credit line — useful for redundancy, less useful if you want to ring-fence
  partner spend
