The Emirates NBD Diners Club is a Mastercard-network card (despite the Diners Club
branding) built around its top earn categories: 5% back in Plus Points at Duty Free
and 2.5% on dining. AED 420 annual fee, AED 12,000 minimum salary and a 1.99% FX fee, with salary transfer
required to qualify.

## Earn rates

<EarnRateTable card="emirates-nbd-diners-club" />

Plus Points convert to Skywards Miles, cashback, or merchant credit at ENBD's
published rates — typically realising around 1¢ per point at the cashback ceiling.
At that rate, the 2.5% dining earn translates to roughly **2.5% effective cashback** on
restaurant spend, with the 5% Duty Free rate the card's standout.

## Fee summary

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Annual fee | AED 420 |
| FX fee | 1.99% |
| Minimum monthly salary | AED 12,000 |
| Salary transfer | **Required** |

The card carries an AED 420 annual fee and the standard 1.99% FX surcharge. The other
cost is the salary-transfer requirement, which locks your monthly income flow with
Emirates NBD.

## Key conditions to know

**Salary transfer requirement:**
- Salary transfer to Emirates NBD is mandatory for approval
- The [salary-transfer tracker](/salary-transfer/) covers current
  switching-bonus offers — stack this application with a transfer bonus
  if you're moving anyway

**Network acceptance:**
- The card runs on the Mastercard network despite the Diners Club branding
- Acceptance is therefore identical to any other Mastercard in the UAE and abroad

**Dining-category definition:**
- Restaurant MCC codes (5811, 5812, 5813, 5814) typically qualify for the 2.5% dining earn
- Some "dining" venues (hotel banquet halls, catering) may MCC-code as
  general merchant — confirm by running a small test transaction

## Best for

UAE residents on AED 12,000+ a month who eat out frequently and are willing to
move their salary to Emirates NBD. The card pays for itself most clearly for
households with AED 2,500+ in monthly restaurant spend.

## Watch out for

- **0.5% base earn** is the weakest non-grocery rate in the ENBD lineup —
  the dining multiplier is the entire value proposition; off-category spend
  underperforms even a basic flat-rate cashback card
- **Salary-transfer lock-in.** Switching banks later requires re-routing
  salary, which is operationally heavier than card cancellation alone
- **Welcome bonus details** are not documented in our snapshot — confirm
  any current offer directly with Emirates NBD before applying
- **Plus Points realise unevenly.** Skywards transfers use unfavourable
  internal rates; cashback at par-value is generally the better redemption
- **Dining-only utility.** Pair with a flat-rate cashback card for
  groceries, fuel, and online retail to avoid the 0.5% drag
