The ADCB Traveller is the bank's flagship travel-leaning Mastercard World Elite,
sold on a single category-cashback hook: 10% back on airline tickets and hotel
stays in any currency, any class, and any destination — capped at AED 1,500
cashback a month, gated by an AED 5,000 minimum monthly spend, and crucially
paired with a 0% foreign-currency fee on every transaction.

## Earn rates

The Traveller is a cashback card, not a miles card — earn is expressed as a
**percentage cashback** on category spend, credited to the card statement:

<EarnRateTable card="adcb-traveller" />

<HotTip>
The 10% rate applies to airline tickets and hotel stays in **any currency** —
including non-AED bookings — which is unusual for a UAE-issued cashback card. But
the AED 1,500 monthly cashback cap means the rate maxes out at AED 15,000 of
qualifying travel spend in a single statement. Above that, the marginal AED
earns nothing, and below AED 5,000 of total monthly spend the cashback doesn't
pay at all. Concentrate qualifying bookings in single-statement months to hit
the cap cleanly.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="adcb-traveller" />

The headline fee position is the foreign-currency rate: **0% FX fee on every
transaction**, confirmed against the ADCB Traveller product page. That is rare
in the UAE market — the prevailing FX rate on premium Visa Infinites and
Mastercard World Elites sits at 2.49% to 3% — and it reframes the card as a
zero-friction overseas swipe on top of the headline travel cashback. Minimum
salary is **AED 20,000 a month** and salary transfer is not required.

## Watch out for

- **The AED 1,500 monthly cap.** Effective cashback maxes out at AED 15,000 of
  qualifying travel spend in a single statement. Book a high-value Business
  fare and the marginal cashback flattens.
- **The AED 5,000 spend gate.** A quiet month under AED 5,000 of total spend
  earns nothing in cashback — even on the travel categories. Plan around the
  qualification, not just the rate.
- **The cashback is the return — not miles.** This is a cashback card; the
  10% return posts as a statement credit, not as ADCB TouchPoints or any
  airline currency. If a Business-class redemption is the goal, a direct-earn
  co-brand is the cleaner buy.
- **Welcome is conditional on the spend gate, not just the fee.** The AED 2,000
  Expedia voucher requires both the annual fee paid and AED 15,000 of card
  spend in the first 60 days — plan around that window.

## Is it worth it?

For a reader who books across multiple airlines and hotel chains, doesn't want
the lock-in of a single co-brand, and would rather see cashback post to the
statement than wait two years for a Business-class redemption: the Traveller is
the strongest travel-cashback rate in market, with 0% FX on every transaction
on top. A single AED 10,000 hotel-and-flight package returns AED 1,000 in
cashback — real money, posted in days rather than months — and the AED 2,000
Expedia voucher on top covers the next booking outright if the spend gate is
cleared inside 60 days.

For the reader whose flying is concentrated on one carrier — Emirates,
Etihad, Qatar — the maths inverts. The
[ENBD Skywards Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite/) and the
[FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) earn directly
into the airline programmes at headline rates that, when redeemed against a
saver Business fare, pay back materially more per dirham than 10% cashback.
The Traveller's edge is breadth, not depth.

## Bottom line

A category-cashback Mastercard World Elite with the strongest published
airline-and-hotel rate in market, a 0% foreign-currency fee on every
transaction, and an AED 2,000 Expedia voucher welcome on the AED 15,000
60-day spend gate. Capped at AED 1,500 a month and gated by AED 5,000 of
monthly spend. **Apply** if you book across multiple carriers and chains
