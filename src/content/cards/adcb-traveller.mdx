The ADCB Traveller is the bank's flagship travel-leaning Visa, sold on a single
category-cashback hook: 10% back on airline tickets and hotel stays in any
currency, any class, and any destination — capped at AED 1,500 cashback a month
and gated by an AED 5,000 minimum monthly spend.

## Earn rates

The Traveller is a cashback card, not a miles card — earn is expressed as a
**percentage cashback** on category spend, credited to the card statement:

<EarnRateTable card="adcb-traveller" />

<HotTip>
The 10% rate applies to airline tickets and hotel stays in **any currency** —
including non-AED bookings — which is unusual for a UAE-issued cashback card. But
the AED 1,500 monthly cashback cap means the rate maxes out at AED 15,000 of
qualifying travel spend in a single statement. Above that, the marginal AED
earns nothing, and below AED 5,000 of total monthly spend the cashback doesn't
pay at all. Concentrate qualifying bookings in single-statement months to hit
the cap cleanly.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="adcb-traveller" />

The L2 schedule records no annual fee on the card. The FX fee is **10%** per
the current ADCB Schedule of Fees — high for a card pitched on overseas spend,
though the 10% airline-and-hotel cashback offsets it on those categories.
Minimum salary is **AED 20,000 a month** and salary transfer is not required.

## Key conditions to know

## Watch out for

- **The AED 1,500 monthly cap.** Effective cashback maxes out at AED 15,000 of
  qualifying travel spend in a single statement. Books a high-value Business
  fare and the marginal cashback flattens.
- **The AED 5,000 spend gate.** A quiet month under AED 5,000 of total spend
  earns nothing in cashback — even on the travel categories. Plan around the
  qualification, not just the rate.
- **10% FX fee.** L2 records the foreign-exchange charge at 10%. The card's
  10% airline and hotel cashback offsets it on those categories — but every
  other non-AED purchase loses 10% to the FX fee with nothing in cashback to
  recover. Verify the FX rate against the current ADCB Schedule before
  using the card abroad outside the travel categories.
- **No miles.** This is a cashback card with TouchPoints branding on the L2
  loyalty field; the 10% return is paid as a statement credit, not as
  ADCB TouchPoints transferable into an airline programme. If a Business-class
  redemption is the goal, a direct-earn co-brand is the cleaner buy.

## Is it worth it?

For a reader who books across multiple airlines and hotel chains, doesn't want
the lock-in of a single co-brand, and would rather see cashback post to the
statement than wait two years for a Business-class redemption: the Traveller is
the strongest travel-cashback rate in market at no annual fee. A single
AED 10,000 hotel-and-flight package returns AED 1,000 in cashback — which is
real money, posted in days rather than months.

For the reader whose flying is concentrated on one carrier — Emirates,
Etihad, Qatar — the maths inverts. The
[ENBD Skywards Infinite](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-infinite/) and the
[FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) earn directly
into the airline programmes at headline rates that, when redeemed against a
saver Business fare, pay back materially more per dirham than 10% cashback.
The Traveller's edge is breadth, not depth.

## Bottom line

A no-fee category-cashback card with the strongest published airline-and-hotel
rate in market, capped at AED 1,500 a month and gated by AED 5,000 of monthly
spend. **Apply** if you book across multiple carriers and chains and want
cashback rather than miles. **Skip** if your loyalty is settled on one airline —
a direct-earn co-brand pays back faster on the same spend.
