The Emirates NBD Marriott Bonvoy World Elite Mastercard is the higher-tier
of ENBD's two Bonvoy co-brands. It carries no annual fee, requires salary
transfer, and is built around a large welcome bonus and direct Bonvoy points
earning on Marriott stays.

## What you actually get

Welcome offer at time of verification is up to 200,000 Bonvoy points:
100,000 on activation plus joining-fee payment, and a further 100,000 on
USD 15,000 (~AED 55,100) of retail spend across the first three statement
cycles. Ongoing earn is 3 Bonvoy points per AED 1 on shopping and on
international (foreign currency) spend, plus 1 per AED 1 on everything else,
and — separately — 6 Bonvoy points per USD 1 at participating Marriott
properties under the Bonvoy programme's own card-member earn layer.
Annual fee is zero. FX fee is 0%. Salary transfer to Emirates NBD is required;
minimum monthly salary AED 25,000. Cardholder gets Bonvoy Silver Elite
status; 15 Elite Night Credits per calendar year are part of the standard
World Elite benefit suite globally — verify the UAE issuance carries this.

## Who should open it

The card matches a household earning AED 25,000+ that takes at least three
Marriott-family stays a year and has the discretionary spend to clear the
USD 15,000 welcome-bonus hurdle inside three statement cycles. Bonvoy
loyalists, GCC business travellers and Address-Hotel-equivalent stay
patterns all index well here.

## Where it falls short

Salary-transfer lock-in is the cost of entry — if there is any chance you
exit Emirates NBD inside the offer tenure, model the clawback before
applying. And the USD-denominated USD 15,000 spend gate is real money: at
the AED 25,000-salary floor, it is roughly two months of total household
discretionary spend, concentrated into three months.

The strongest welcome bonus in the UAE co-brand range right now; the long-
term hold case depends on your Marriott footprint.
