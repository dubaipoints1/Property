<HotTip>
Weekend means Friday–Saturday in the UAE. The 2.5% rate applies only on weekend
spend at qualifying lifestyle partners; weekday spend drops to 0.5% — well below
the UAE cashback floor. Plus Points convert to Skywards Miles at internal rates
that are typically less efficient than direct-earning Skywards cards; cashback at
par is usually the better redemption.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-go4it-platinum" />

The card is currently advertised at **AED 0 annual fee** under ENBD's running
promotion. The KFS Schedule of Charges standard renewal is **AED 208.95** (incl. VAT)
— that's what to budget for once the promo expires. Monthly interest is 3.49% (~42%
APR), FX is 1.99%.

## Perks worth using

The Go4it Platinum is sold as a perks card, not an earn card, so the maths has
to come from the lifestyle benefits — not the 0.5% weekday rate. We've put a
per-benefit Value to me on each so you can see what the perk stack actually
delivers against the standard AED 208.95 renewal once the AED 0 promo lapses:

### BOGO VOX Cinemas + 25% off cinema F&B

VOX standard tickets are AED 50–60 in Dubai; the BOGO clears roughly AED 55 per
paired visit, and the 25% off F&B saves another AED 15–25 on a typical
popcorn-and-drinks combo. Light use (one paired visit per month): AED 55 ticket
+ AED 20 F&B × 12 = **Value to me: AED 900/year**. Heavy use (weekly paired
visit): AED 55 + AED 20 × 52 = **Value to me: AED 3,900/year**. The mid case
(two visits a month) lands at **AED 1,800/year** — already 8× the standard
renewal.

### Gold Class Metro access

Dubai Metro Gold Class fares are roughly double the Silver Class fare — AED 8.50
vs AED 5.00 on a Zone-2 trip is the routine delta, so call it AED 3.50 extra per
ride. The value here is being *able* to ride Gold Class directly with the card,
not a discount — but if you'd ride it anyway, the convenience of skipping the
Nol top-up at peak time is worth something. Daily Gold Class commuter (2 rides
× 22 working days × 12): **Value to me: AED 0/year as a discount**, but a real
quality-of-life benefit on a packed Red Line at 8am. Score this honestly at zero
unless you'd pay for the convenience.

### Nol card functionality

The chip is a working Nol card — Metro, RTA buses, parking meters, water bus.
The value is the saved AED 25 plastic-Nol cost + the cashflow benefit of not
pre-loading a separate Nol balance. **Value to me: AED 25 in year one**, then
nominal. The one free Dubai Ferry ride per month (cardholder + 1 guest, AED 50
per pair) adds **AED 600/year** if you actually use it — most readers won't.

### The Entertainer BOGO

ENBD's Entertainer integration is narrower than the standalone app. Scope is
limited to a partner list that refreshes; treat as a bonus, not a planned saving.
Two paired meals a month at an AED 100 saving each: **Value to me:
AED 2,400/year** if you'll actively check the offer list before booking. Most
holders use this once or twice and forget it — realistic mid case **AED 200–
600/year**.

### Life insurance + golf

AED 100,000 complimentary life insurance has a stand-alone retail value of
roughly AED 150–250/year if you'd buy a comparable term policy — though most
readers already hold life cover through an employer or family policy and would
not buy this separately. **Value to me: AED 0–200/year**. Up to 40% off green
fees at 100+ courses globally is meaningful only if you golf — at AED 200 saved
per round, four rounds a year is **AED 800/year**; non-golfers, **AED 0**.

For a UAE resident who actually uses the cinema and Entertainer perks twice a
month and rides Gold Class regularly, the perk stack delivers roughly
**AED 2,500–3,000/year** against the AED 208.95 standard renewal — and **pure
upside** during the current AED 0 promo. Heavy cinema + Entertainer use can push
the stack past **AED 4,000/year**. Don't hold the card for the points engine;
hold it because the perk maths clears the fee 10–15× over for the right user.

## Go4it Platinum vs Go4it Gold

<CardComparison cards={["emirates-nbd-go4it-platinum", "emirates-nbd-go4it-gold"]} />

The Platinum's upgrade over the Gold is a perks upgrade, not a points one — the base
weekday earn rate is similar, and the headline difference is the VOX BOGO, Gold Class
Metro access, The Entertainer BOGO, and a higher life-insurance cover (AED 100k vs
AED 75k). The Gold costs AED 103.95 a year; the Platinum is currently AED 0 under
promo but reverts to AED 208.95 at standard renewal. **The decision rule:** if you'll
use VOX BOGO and Gold Class Metro often enough to clear the AED 105 standard-renewal
gap, take the Platinum. If not, the Gold is the cheaper transit-and-life card.

## Watch out for

- **0.5% weekday base earn is genuinely thin.** This is a perks card, not a rewards card —
  earn on a separate card and use this for cinema, Metro and Nol top-ups. Conversion to
  Skywards Miles is unfavourable; redeem Plus Points as cashback at par.
- **Promo fee may expire.** The advertised AED 0 is a current promotion; the standard
  renewal is AED 208.95. Verify the active fee on the application page.
- **3.49% monthly interest** (~42% APR) is at the upper end of the UAE range — revolve a
  balance for one month and the year's earn evaporates.

## Bottom line

A perks card priced as a transit-and-cinema convenience, not a rewards engine. If you
commute on Dubai Metro and will use VOX BOGO and the Gold Class fare weekly, the
Platinum is the cheapest way to consolidate those perks while the AED 0 promo holds —
the VOX BOGO alone clears AED 60–80 per visit at standard pricing. **Apply** if you'll
use VOX BOGO and Gold Class Metro often. **Skip** if you want a points engine — the
0.5% weekday rate is below the UAE cashback floor, the [Gold sibling](/cards/emirates-nbd-go4it-gold/)
is cheaper for the same base earn, and a flat-rate cashback card returns more on
general spend.

**Pay smart:** pair the Platinum with the [CBD One](/cards/cbd-one/) free-for-life card
for AED-denominated cashback on everyday spend, or the
[Skywards Signature](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-signature/) for travel-leaning spend.
