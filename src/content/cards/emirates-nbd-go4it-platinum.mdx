<HotTip>
Weekend means Friday–Saturday in the UAE. The 2.5% rate applies only on weekend
spend at qualifying lifestyle partners; weekday spend drops to 0.5% — well below
the UAE cashback floor. Plus Points convert to Skywards Miles at internal rates
that are typically less efficient than direct-earning Skywards cards; cashback at
par is usually the better redemption.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-go4it-platinum" />

The card is currently advertised at **AED 0 annual fee** under ENBD's running
promotion. The KFS Schedule of Charges standard renewal is **AED 208.95** (incl. VAT)
— that's what to budget for once the promo expires. Monthly interest is 3.49% (~42%
APR), FX is 1.99%.

## Go4it Platinum vs Go4it Gold

<CardComparison cards={["emirates-nbd-go4it-platinum", "emirates-nbd-go4it-gold"]} />

The Platinum's upgrade over the Gold is a perks upgrade, not a points one — the base
weekday earn rate is similar, and the headline difference is the VOX BOGO, Gold Class
Metro access, The Entertainer BOGO, and a higher life-insurance cover (AED 100k vs
AED 75k). The Gold costs AED 103.95 a year; the Platinum is currently AED 0 under
promo but reverts to AED 208.95 at standard renewal. **The decision rule:** if you'll
use VOX BOGO and Gold Class Metro often enough to clear the AED 105 standard-renewal
gap, take the Platinum. If not, the Gold is the cheaper transit-and-life card.

## Watch out for

- **0.5% weekday base earn is genuinely thin.** This is a perks card, not a rewards
  card — earn on a separate card and use this for cinema, Metro and Nol top-ups.
- **Promo fee may expire.** The advertised AED 0 is a current promotion; the standard
  renewal is AED 208.95. Verify the active fee on the application page.
- **Plus Points realise poorly.** Conversion to Skywards Miles is unfavourable;
  cashback at par is typically the better redemption path.
- **3.49% monthly interest** (~42% APR) is at the upper end of the UAE range —
  revolve a balance for one month and the year's earn evaporates.

## Is it worth it?

If you commute on Dubai Metro and want a single card that handles Nol, Gold Class fares,
RTA parking and the water bus — and you'll use VOX BOGO and the cinema F&B discount
weekly — the Platinum is genuinely the cheapest way to consolidate those perks while
the AED 0 promo holds. The Gold Class Metro perk alone removes the friction of carrying
a separate Nol card; the VOX BOGO clears AED 60–80 per visit at standard pricing.

The card is the wrong choice for a points engine. 0.5% on weekday spend is below the UAE
cashback floor, and ENBD Plus Points realise poorly compared with direct-earning Skywards
cards or flat-rate cashback. Pair the Platinum with a cashback or miles card for general
spend; hold this one for the cinema, the transit, and the lifestyle perks.

**Pay smart:** for general spend, the [CBD One](/cards/cbd-one/) free-for-life card
returns more on app spend, and the [Skywards Signature](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-signature/)
earns better on travel-leaning spend.

## Bottom line

A perks card priced as a transit-and-cinema convenience, not a rewards engine. **Apply**
if you'll use VOX BOGO and Gold Class Metro often. **Skip** if you want a points engine
— the [Gold sibling](/cards/emirates-nbd-go4it-gold/) is cheaper for the same base earn,
or step out to a flat-rate cashback card.
