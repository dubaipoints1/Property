The FAB Etihad Guest Infinite is FAB's co-branded card with Etihad Airways,
issued on the Visa Infinite tier. It targets Etihad Guest members who do their
flying — and a meaningful share of their spending — internationally.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 10 Etihad Guest Miles per AED 1 on international (foreign
currency) spend, 2 miles per AED 1 on travel, and 1 mile per AED 1 on
everything else. The 10x international rate is the structural reason to hold
this card; it materially out-earns a generic Etihad-partner spend on most UAE-
issued cards. Annual fee is AED 2,500. FX fee is 2.49%, which is in normal
range for a premium UAE Visa. Salary transfer is not required (minimum salary
AED 2,500), which is unusual for a card with this earn ceiling.

## Who should open it

The card matches a reader who: (a) is loyal to Etihad Guest rather than
Skywards, (b) spends AED 3,000+ a month in foreign currency through online
merchants or travel, and (c) wants to skip the salary-transfer commitment. AED
30,000+ monthly household earners who travel through AUH at least quarterly
will recover the fee through the international earn alone.

## Where it falls short

The 1x domestic floor is unremarkable — for AED-denominated daily spend you
are leaving rewards on the table versus a category-bonus cashback card. The
AED 2,500 fee bites without a first-year waiver, and the card's value is so
concentrated in the international multiplier that an expat who shifts spending
back to AED for a year sees their effective return collapse.

A specialist card for Etihad-loyal, internationally-spending residents; ignore
otherwise.
