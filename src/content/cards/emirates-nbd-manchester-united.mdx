club. It earns Red Points redeemable inside the club's loyalty programme — official
merchandise, branded match experiences, and (subject to quota and availability) match
tickets at Old Trafford.

## Status — closed to new applicants

Emirates NBD closed this card to new applicants on **1 June 2025**. Existing cardholders
retain all benefits and continue to earn at the published rates. This review is kept on the
site as a reference for current holders and for the historical record. If you are looking
for an active ENBD co-brand from the same wallet, the
[Skywards Signature](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-signature/) and the
[U by Emaar Family](/cards/emirates-nbd-u-by-emaar-family/) are the closest live
alternatives.

## Earn rates

All earn pays as **Red Points** — a closed-loop currency redeemable only inside Manchester
United's own loyalty catalogue. There is no airline-transfer route, no cashback escape, and
no statement-credit option:

<HotTip>
A household putting **AED 1,000 a month** through sports-goods retailers in the UAE (Sun &
Sand Sports, Sport In Life, Go Sport) accrues **10,000 Red Points** a year on that line
alone; a season's replica kit at the club store runs roughly 50,000 Red Points at standard
conversion. Dining at 5x is the secondary lever — AED 2,000 a month of dining adds another
10,000 Red Points a year. Outside those two categories the card is a 1% earner.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-manchester-united" />

The card carries **no annual fee**, a **1.99% foreign-currency markup** in line with the
ENBD Plus Points range, and a 3.49% monthly retail finance charge. Clear the statement in
full each month — revolve a balance and the year's Red Points evaporate under the interest
charge many times over. All published fees include 5% VAT.

## Watch out for

- **Closed-loop currency.** Red Points only spend inside the Manchester United catalogue.
  If interest in the club fades, the accumulating balance is stranded — there is no clean
  exit to cashback, miles or statement credit.
- **1x base earn is unremarkable.** Value concentrates in the two bonus categories
  (sports-goods at 10x, dining at 5x) and the underlying fandom. Pair the card with a
  flat-rate cashback card for everything outside those lines.
- **FX markup at 1.99%.** The 2x international rate partly offsets the markup but does not
  eliminate it. This is not a travel-spend card despite the foreign-currency multiplier.
- **No new applications.** If you are reading this looking to open one, the answer is the
  Skywards Signature or U by Emaar Family — neither earns Red Points, but both are live.

## Bottom line

A fan card — exactly what it says on the tin — and one that is now a hold-only product for
existing cardholders. **Hold** if you still buy Manchester United merchandise or travel to
Old Trafford; the 10x at sports-goods retailers and 5x on dining quietly compound on spend
you were doing anyway. **Move on** if the fandom has faded — there is no escape valve on
Red Points other than redeeming inside the United catalogue, and a no-fee cashback or
Skywards card recovers the spend instead.
