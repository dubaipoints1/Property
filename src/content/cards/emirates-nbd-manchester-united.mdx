The Emirates NBD Manchester United Credit Card is the bank's co-brand with
the football club. It earns Red Points redeemable inside the club's
loyalty programme — official merchandise, branded match experiences,
and (depending on quota and availability) match tickets at Old Trafford.

## Status — closed to new applicants

Emirates NBD closed this card to new applicants on **1 June 2025**.
Existing cardholders retain all benefits and continue to earn at the
published rates. This review is kept on the site as a reference for
current holders and for the historical record; if you are looking for
an active ENBD co-brand from the same wallet, the
[Skywards Signature](/cards/emirates-nbd-skywards-signature/) and the
[U by Emaar Family](/cards/emirates-nbd-u-by-emaar-family/) are the
closest live alternatives.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 10 Red Points per AED 1 at sports-goods retailers,
5 per AED 1 on dining, 2 per AED 1 on international (foreign currency)
spend, and 1 per AED 1 on everything else. The card carries **no
annual fee**, a 1.99% foreign-currency markup per L2, and a 3.49%
monthly retail finance charge — clear the statement in full each
month or the year's Red Points evaporate.

## How the earn translates into club spend

Red Points redeem inside Manchester United's loyalty catalogue —
not as cashback, not as Skywards Miles, not as statement credit.
A household putting AED 1,000 a month through sports-goods retailers
in the UAE (Sun & Sand Sports, Sport In Life, Go Sport) accrues
10,000 Red Points a year on that line alone; a season's replica kit
at the club store runs roughly 50,000 Red Points at standard
conversion. Dining at 5x is the secondary lever — AED 2,000 a month
of dining spend adds another 10,000 a year. The 2x rate on
international spend partly offsets the FX fee but does not eliminate
it: net of the 1.99% markup, foreign-currency spend earns roughly
0.01% of additional value beyond the points.

## Who should hold it

The card matches a Manchester United supporter who already buys club
merchandise — replica kits run AED 350–500 a season, so any household
that re-kits annually has a clear point-utilisation path. It works
particularly well for fans who travel to England at least once for a
match, where Red Points unlock the kind of curated experiences cash
alone won't.

## Where it falls short

Red Points are entirely closed-loop. If the cardholder loses interest
in the club, changes loyalty, or simply stops buying merchandise, the
accumulating points are stranded — there is no clean exit to cashback,
miles or statement credit. The 1x base earn is unremarkable for any
non-bonus spend, so the value is concentrated in the two bonus
categories and the underlying fandom.

## Bottom line

A fan card — exactly what it says on the tin. Excellent for the right
holder while the card remains in their wallet; pointless for everyone
else, and closed to new applications regardless. **Hold** if you are
an existing cardholder who still buys club merchandise. **Move on** if
the fandom has faded — there is no escape valve on Red Points other
than redeeming inside the United catalogue.
