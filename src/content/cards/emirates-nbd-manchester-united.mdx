The Emirates NBD Manchester United Credit Card is the bank's co-brand with
the football club. It earns Red Points redeemable inside the club's
loyalty programme — official merchandise, branded match experiences,
and (depending on quota and availability) match tickets at Old Trafford.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 10 Red Points per AED 1 at sports-goods retailers,
5 per AED 1 on dining, 2 per AED 1 on international (foreign currency)
spend, and 1 per AED 1 on everything else. Annual fee is zero.
FX fee is 1.99% per L2 — overseas spend clips most of the 2×
international earn before any Red Point is applied. Salary transfer
is not required; minimum monthly salary AED 5,000.

**Status note:** Emirates NBD closed this card to new applicants on
1 June 2025. Existing cardholders retain all benefits. This review is
kept on the site as a reference for current holders and for the
record.

## Who should open it

The card matches a Manchester United supporter who already buys club
merchandise — replica kits run AED 350–500 a season, so any household
that re-kits annually has a clear point-utilisation path. It works
particularly well for fans who travel to England at least once for a
match, where Red Points unlock the kind of curated experiences cash alone
won't.

## Where it falls short

Red Points are entirely closed-loop. If the cardholder loses interest in
the club, changes loyalty, or simply stops buying merchandise, the
accumulating points are stranded. The 1x base earn is unremarkable, so
the value is concentrated in the two bonus categories and the underlying
fandom.

A fan card — exactly what it says on the tin. Excellent for the right
holder; pointless for everyone else.
