The Emirates NBD Visa Flexi is the most multi-category no-fee card in the ENBD
lineup — points across groceries, travel, fuel, and entertainment, with a flat
1% catch-all rate on everything else. No annual fee, no FX fee, AED 12,000
minimum salary.

## Earn rates

| Spend category | Plus Points per AED 1 |
|---|---|
| Groceries | **4** |
| Travel | **3** |
| Fuel | **2** |
| Entertainment | 1 |
| Everything else | 1 |

Plus Points convert to Skywards Miles, cashback, or merchant credit at ENBD's
published rates. At the cashback ceiling (~1¢ per point) the headline rates
translate to roughly **4% effective cashback on groceries, 3% on travel, 2%
on fuel** — competitive across all three categories for a no-fee card.

## Fee summary

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Annual fee | AED 0 |
| FX fee | 0% |
| Minimum monthly salary | AED 12,000 |
| Salary transfer | Not required |

The fee profile is the second strength after the category multipliers — zero
ongoing cost and zero FX surcharge.

## Key conditions to know

**Category MCC mapping:**
- **Groceries**: typically MCC 5411 (grocery stores and supermarkets) — LuLu,
  Carrefour, Spinneys, Choithrams all qualify
- **Travel**: MCCs 3000–3299 (airlines), 3300–3499 (car rental), 3500–3999
  (hotels), 4111 (transit), 4112 (rail), 4131 (bus), 4411 (cruise),
  4511 (airlines), 4722 (travel agencies)
- **Fuel**: 5541 / 5542 — ADNOC, ENOC, EPPCO transactions code correctly
- **Entertainment**: typically MCCs 7832 (cinemas), 7922 (theatres),
  7929 (live entertainment), 7991 (recreation services)

Verify mapping with small test transactions before assuming a category
applies — some hybrid merchants (e.g., hotel restaurants) code as the
parent merchant's MCC rather than dining.

**Plus Points redemption:**
- Cashback at par-value is the cleanest redemption path
- Skywards transfers use unfavourable internal rates — typically 3:1 or
  worse
- Points expire after 36 months of inactivity in some programmes — confirm
  current Plus Points expiry rules

## Best for

UAE households on AED 12,000+ a month who spread spending across groceries,
travel, and fuel and want a single no-fee card to anchor those categories.
The card is one of the strongest default-card candidates in the ENBD lineup
for typical household spending patterns.

## Watch out for

- **Plus Points realisation is uneven.** Always redeem as cashback unless you
  have a specific Skywards goal — the internal transfer rate erodes value
- **Welcome bonus is undocumented** at our last verification — confirm
  current offers directly with the issuer
- **Entertainment is at the base rate.** "Entertainment" earns at 1x, not a
  bonus rate — the LuLu 247 Platinum or Go4it Platinum carry stronger
  entertainment multipliers
- **No category cap published** at our last verification — large
  single-category months may trip an undocumented monthly ceiling. Verify
  with the issuer if you plan a big-ticket purchase in a bonus category
