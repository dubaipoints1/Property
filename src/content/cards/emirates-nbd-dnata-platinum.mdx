The Emirates NBD dnata Platinum Credit Card is the no-fee tier of the dnata
co-brand. It earns dnata Travel Points redeemable against bookings made
through dnata's UAE channels and is open to salaried residents from
AED 5,000 monthly without a salary-transfer commitment.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 10 dnata Travel Points per AED 1 on travel, 5 per AED 1 on
shopping, and 1 per AED 1 on everything else. Annual fee is zero. FX fee
is 0% — useful for foreign-currency hotel bookings outside dnata where
the points engine does not apply but the FX advantage still does. No
salary transfer is required (minimum salary AED 5,000). Points post to
the dnata loyalty account and redeem against the agency's published
conversion table.

## Who should open it

The card matches a household that books at least AED 15,000–30,000 of travel
a year through dnata. A no-fee, low-salary-floor entry into the dnata
ecosystem makes this an appropriate first travel card for residents inside
the first three years of their UAE move — particularly those who already
book package holidays through dnata branches in shopping malls.

## Where it falls short

dnata Travel Points are useful only inside dnata's ecosystem; if your
travel pattern shifts towards direct airline bookings or international OTAs,
the loyalty value evaporates. The 1x base earn means the card is not a
sensible default for non-travel, non-shopping spend.

The right entry-tier dnata card; pair with a Plus-Points-earning ENBD card
for groceries and fuel.
