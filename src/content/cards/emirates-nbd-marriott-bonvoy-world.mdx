The Emirates NBD Marriott Bonvoy World Mastercard is the lighter sibling of ENBD's
two Bonvoy co-brands — a direct-earn hotel card built for the AED 12,000-to-25,000
salary band that wants Marriott Bonvoy points without an annual fee, a salary
transfer, or the World Elite's heavier spend gates.

## Earn rates

ENBD quotes Bonvoy earn per **USD 1** of eligible spend, not per AED. At USD 1 ≈
AED 3.67, 1.5 Bonvoy points per USD 1 on international and general retail spend
resolves to roughly 0.41 points per AED 1; the 3-points-per-USD top rate at
Marriott properties resolves to ~0.82 points per AED 1:

<EarnRateTable card="emirates-nbd-marriott-bonvoy-world" />

Points post directly to your Marriott Bonvoy account — no transfer step, no
intermediate currency.

turns this card's anniversary 35,000-point Free Night Award and 10 Elite Night

to 50,000 with 15,000 of your own Bonvoy points and you can book most off-peak
Marriott-family Palm properties, then chain a four-night cash stay around it and
let the elite-night credits stack toward Gold.

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-marriott-bonvoy-world" />

The card is currently advertised as **Free for Life** — AED 0 joining and annual
fee with no spend threshold to clear. The two costs to watch are a 1.99%
foreign-currency fee on every non-AED transaction and the standard ENBD Schedule
of Charges figure of AED 315 for renewal, which is the contractual fall-back if
ENBD ever withdraws the Free for Life campaign. All fees include 5% VAT.

## Welcome bonus breakdown

<WelcomeBonusCallout card="emirates-nbd-marriott-bonvoy-world" />

The welcome lands as **2 Free Night Awards capped at 35,000 Bonvoy points each**,
issued on cumulative **USD 6,000 (~AED 22,000) of retail spend within the first
three billing statements**. That is roughly AED 7,300 a month of card spend — a
realistic target for a household at the AED 12,000-salary floor that routes
groceries, utilities and discretionary spend through the card. Each certificate
can be topped up with up to 15,000 of your own Bonvoy points to unlock a
50,000-point night — useful because the 35,000 cap no longer stretches to
peak-season Dubai Ritz-Carlton or St. Regis properties. At our [2.5-fils-per-point
Bonvoy baseline](/airlines/marriott-bonvoy/), each Free Night Award redeemed at
its 35,000-point cap is worth around AED 875; topped up to 50,000 it lands closer
to AED 1,250.

## What you get — per-benefit value

All Bonvoy figures use the [2.5-fils-per-point baseline](/airlines/marriott-bonvoy/)
published on the programme overview. Earn is quoted per **USD 1** of spend, so every
AED figure below converts at USD 1 = AED 3.6725 — read the divisor before comparing
this card with an AED-quoted earner. The fee to beat is AED 0 on the current Free
for Life offer.

### Welcome offer — 2 Free Night Awards

Two certificates capped at 35,000 Bonvoy points each, on USD 6,000 (~AED 22,000)
of retail spend in the first three statements.
**Value to me: AED 1,750, one-time** — 70,000 points at the 2.5-fil baseline,
redeemed at the 35,000-point cap. Topping each certificate up with 15,000 of your
own points unlocks 50,000-point nights, but the top-up points are yours, not the
card's, so we don't credit them here.

### Anniversary Free Night Award

One 35,000-point certificate on each card anniversary.
**Value to me: AED 875/year** at the baseline — assuming you actually burn it
inside its validity window on a night priced at or near the cap.

### Marriott Bonvoy Silver Elite status

Automatic on activation. Silver's headline is a 10% points bonus on stays — on
AED 10,000 a year of paid Marriott stays (~USD 2,720 at 10 base points per USD 1),
the bonus is ~2,700 points.
**Value to me: roughly AED 70/year.** Priority late check-out and member rates
carry no published cash value; we book them at AED 0.

### 10 Elite Night Credits a year

A status accelerator, not a benefit with a cash price.
**Value to me: AED 0 in isolation** — the credits only pay if they tip you from
Silver to Gold (25 nights) on a year you'd otherwise miss it. If they do, the
value sits in Gold's stay bonus, not in this line.

### Bonvoy earn on card spend

Base earn of 1.5 points per USD 1 works out to ~0.41 points per AED 1 — about a
1.0% return at the baseline. On AED 60,000 a year of general retail spend
(AED 5,000 a month): ~24,500 points.
**Value to me: roughly AED 610/year.** The 3-points-per-USD rate at Marriott
properties (~2.0% return) adds ~AED 200/year on AED 10,000 of annual Marriott
stays charged to the card. Groceries (0.375/USD ≈ 0.26%) and fuel and utilities
(0.15/USD ≈ 0.10%) are filler rates — route that spend elsewhere. On
foreign-currency spend the 1.99% FX fee exceeds the ~1.0% earn value, so
international earn is net negative at the baseline; EU/UK spend earns at 50% of
the listed rates, which makes it worse still.

**Total package:** for a holder clearing the welcome, year one sums to roughly
**AED 2,400–3,300** at the baseline (welcome AED 1,750 + earn ~AED 610–800 +
Silver ~AED 70, with the first anniversary certificate landing at month twelve).
Ongoing years run about **AED 1,500–1,700/year** (anniversary night + earn +
Silver) against an AED 0 fee — all yield, no fee to recover, which is the whole
argument for this card at the AED 12,000–25,000 salary band.

## Watch out for

- **USD-quoted earn rates.** ENBD's marketing reads "3 Marriott Bonvoy points per
  USD 1" — comparing like-for-like with AED-quoted cashback or miles cards needs a
  3.67 divisor. The headline 3x at Marriott is ~0.82 points per AED 1.
- **The 1.99% FX fee.** Despite the up-to-1.5-points-per-USD international earn,
  the FX cost is roughly the same magnitude as the points value — net return on
  foreign-currency spend is close to zero at our Bonvoy valuation.
- **35,000-point certificate cap.** Peak-season Dubai Marriott rates routinely
  clear 50,000–70,000 points a night. Budget either the 15,000-point top-up or an
  off-peak (May to early September) booking window.
- **Devaluation risk.** Marriott has moved off published award charts since 2022.
  Treat Bonvoy points as a spending currency, not a savings one — bank balances
  drift in real value with each chart move.
- **"Free for Life" is a campaign, not a contract.** ENBD's standard Schedule of
  Charges lists an AED 315 renewal for this card. The Free for Life pitch can be
  withdrawn on future cardholder cohorts — existing cardholders are usually
  grandfathered, but verify on application.

## Bottom line

For a Marriott-loyal UAE resident in the AED 12,000–25,000 salary band the
math is straightforward: zero annual fee, no salary-transfer commitment,
automatic Silver Elite status, 10 Elite Night Credits a year, and two Free
Night Awards in the first three months if you can clear USD 6,000 of card
spend. At our 2.5-fils-per-point baseline the two welcome certificates are
worth roughly AED 1,750 redeemed at cap — pure upside against an AED 0 fee.

**Apply** at the AED 12,000–25,000 band if you'll stay at a Marriott property
a few times a year. **Upgrade to the
[World Elite](/cards/emirates-nbd-marriott-bonvoy-world-elite/)** once your
salary clears AED 25,000 and you can route USD 15,000 of spend in three
months — its 200,000-point welcome earns back the AED 1,575 joining fee
several times over in year one.
