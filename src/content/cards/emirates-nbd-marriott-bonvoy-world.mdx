The Emirates NBD Marriott Bonvoy World Mastercard is the lower-tier Bonvoy
co-brand from ENBD. It earns Bonvoy points directly into the cardholder's
Marriott Bonvoy account, with no annual fee and no salary-transfer
commitment — a meaningful difference from the World Elite sibling.

## What you actually get

Earn rates are 5 Bonvoy points per AED 1 on shopping, 5 per AED 1 on
international (foreign currency) spend, and 1 per AED 1 on everything else,
on top of any earnings cardholders receive directly from Marriott when
paying at Bonvoy properties. Annual fee is zero. FX fee is 0%, making
foreign-currency online spend genuinely productive. Salary transfer not
required; minimum monthly salary AED 12,000. Standard World Mastercard
benefits apply at the network level.

## Who should open it

The card matches a resident in the AED 12,000–25,000 monthly band who
already books Marriott stays — particularly the family that takes a few
weekend staycations at Address-equivalent properties and one or two
international Marriott stays a year. It also works as a foreign-currency
spend card for anyone who wants Bonvoy as their travel currency without
salary-transfer commitments.

## Where it falls short

The point density is meaningfully lower than the World Elite — no welcome
bonus of any consequence, no automatic Bonvoy status, no Elite Night
Credits. Combined with Bonvoy's history of devaluations, the long-term
AED-equivalent value here is modest unless you spend heavily in the bonus
categories.

A respectable no-fee Bonvoy card; the World Elite is the destination
product once your salary clears AED 25,000.
