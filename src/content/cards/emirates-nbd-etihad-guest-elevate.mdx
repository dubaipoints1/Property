The Emirates NBD Etihad Guest Visa Elevate is the middle rung in ENBD's Etihad
co-brand ladder, sitting between the AED 12,000-salary Inspire and the AED
30,000-salary FAB Etihad Guest Infinite from a competing issuer. It earns
Etihad Guest Miles directly into the cardholder's account at the AED 30,000
salary bar, with salary transfer required.

<HotTip>
The Elevate's headline earn rate of **5 Etihad Guest Miles per AED 10** on
Etihad airfare, hotels and dining is below both the ENBD Inspire's and the
FAB Etihad Guest Infinite's **7 miles per AED 10** at the same partner
categories — the Elevate is a welcome-bonus product, not an ongoing-earn
product. If the **200,000-mile welcome** is the reason you're applying, model
your year-one return on that bonus alone; if you expect to lean on the ongoing
earn rate, the sibling cards earn 40% more per dirham on Etihad partner spend.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-etihad-guest-elevate" />

The annual fee is **AED 1,575** including VAT, with no published first-year
waiver. The **1.99%** FX fee is below the 2.49% norm on most premium UAE Visa
Infinite cards in market — useful for an expat whose foreign-currency spend is
meaningful. Minimum salary is **AED 30,000 a month** and salary transfer is
required — a material differentiator from both the ENBD Inspire (no transfer
required) and the FAB Etihad Guest Infinite (no transfer required at the same
salary bar).

## Key conditions to know

<dl class="dp-conditions">
  <dt>Welcome bonus mechanics</dt>
  <dd>Up to 200,000 Etihad Guest Miles on activation and qualifying spend per the ENBD product page. At Etihad's published saver charts that's enough for a long-haul Business one-way (AUH → JFK from ~88,000 miles per the <a href="/airlines/etihad-guest/">Etihad Guest sweet-spot list</a>) with substantial headroom remaining for a second redemption.</dd>

  <dt>Redemption voucher</dt>
  <dd>50% Etihad Guest Miles redemption-discount voucher on every AED 150,000 of annual spend — useful for stretching a long-haul Business redemption further if you hit the spend bar. The Inspire offers 25% on AED 100,000 and the FAB Infinite offers 75% on AED 200,000; the Elevate's voucher sits in between on both rate and spend threshold.</dd>

  <dt>Tier Mile earn</dt>
  <dd>2.5 Etihad Tier Miles per AED 10 spent on all categories. Card spend cannot satisfy the from-flying minimum on any Etihad Guest tier (10,000 for Silver, 20,000 for Gold, 40,000 for Platinum) — see the <a href="/airlines/etihad-guest/">Etihad Guest programme guide</a> for the Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Emerald tier ladder.</dd>

  <dt>Express Miles add-on</dt>
  <dd>Optional opt-in at AED 250 a month earns 50% more miles across all categories. Adds AED 3,000 a year to the running cost — only pays back at sustained six-figure annual spend concentrated outside the Etihad partner categories.</dd>

  <dt>Lounge access</dt>
  <dd>Complimentary access to 1,000+ airport lounges worldwide via the Visa Airport Companion app. Independent of Etihad Guest tier status. Concierge service and valet parking at selected Abu Dhabi locations also included on the Visa Infinite chassis.</dd>

  <dt>Eligibility</dt>
  <dd>Minimum salary AED 30,000/month; salary transfer required; UAE residency required; salaried employment.</dd>

## Watch out for

- **Salary transfer is mandatory.** Unlike the Inspire and the FAB Infinite,
  the Elevate requires committing your salary to ENBD — a banking decision
  that affects your salary-transfer-incentive eligibility elsewhere. Check the
  [salary-transfer tracker](/salary-transfer/) before applying.
- **Ongoing earn trails the siblings.** 5 miles per AED 10 on Etihad spend is
  40% below the 7 miles per AED 10 that both the ENBD Inspire (at half the
  salary bar) and the FAB Etihad Guest Infinite (at the same salary bar) pay.
- **2 miles per AED 10 on general spend** is roughly 0.2 miles per AED 1 — a
  flat cashback card pays back more on UAE in-store spend.
- **Tier Mile cap and from-flying floors.** The 2.5 Tier Miles per AED 10
  rate is uncapped within the card's terms but still cannot fulfil the
  from-flying minimums each Etihad Guest tier requires — you cannot card-spend
  your way to status.
- **Etihad-loyal only.** A Dubai-based Skywards flyer earns more per dirham
  on an [ENBD or FAB Skywards co-brand](/airlines/skywards/).

## Bottom line

The Elevate's claim to this market is the 200,000-mile welcome and the
convenience of holding the card on the same ENBD relationship as your salary
account. For an Abu Dhabi or northern-emirate resident who flies Etihad two
or three times a year and is willing to transfer salary to ENBD, the welcome
alone clears the AED 1,575 annual fee several times over at any reasonable
Etihad Guest Miles valuation and funds a long-haul Business one-way at saver
rate with headroom to spare.

If you'd rather keep your salary elsewhere, the
[FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) earns 7 miles
per AED 10 on Etihad spend (40% more) at the same fee with no transfer
requirement. At AED 12k–25k salary, the
[ENBD Inspire](/cards/emirates-nbd-etihad-guest-inspire/) earns the same 7
per AED 10 on Etihad at half the ongoing fee. If neither sibling's tradeoff
appeals, take the Elevate; otherwise it's the wrong card.
