invitation to existing Priority Banking customers as part of the wider wealth-management package.

the relationship brings around the card, not in the standalone earn rate.

## Earn rates

Plus Points earn is on every transaction, with small category boosts on groceries and insurance
and a thin earn on fuel and utilities:

<HotTip>
The base earn is **2 Plus Points per AED 100** of general spend — modest for a Visa Infinite at
this fee tier. Category rates of **0.4% on groceries and insurance** and **0.2% on fuel and
utilities** apply on the same Plus Points basis, capped at the standard per-statement maximum.
This card is not bought for the earn rate; it is bought because you are inside Priority Banking
and want the in-ecosystem Visa Infinite.

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-priority-banking-visa-infinite" />

The annual fee is **AED 1,575**. The Priority Banking relationship sometimes waives the card fee
at the bank's discretion for higher-tier customers — confirm with your relationship manager
whether your tier currently qualifies. The **1.99% foreign-currency fee** applies to every non-AED
card transaction; Priority Banking's preferential FX rates apply to outbound transfers and currency
exchange, not to card transactions.

## What you get — per-benefit value

The earn is denominated in ENBD Plus Points, which carry no published AED redemption
value — so the countable value on this card sits in the relationship perks, counted
against the AED 1,575 fee.

### Plus Points earn (2% general / 0.4% groceries / 0.2% fuel)

**Value to me: not computable — conversion unpublished.** ENBD publishes no Plus
Points→AED rate. Whatever the portal pays at redemption is the real figure; don't read
the 2% as dirhams.

### Up to 4 valet visits a month (Abu Dhabi)

At the AED 50–80 mall-valet band we use across reviews, the full 48-visit ration is
**Value to me: AED 2,400–3,840/year**; a realistic once-a-month user books
**AED 600–960/year**. Selected Abu Dhabi locations only — **AED 0** for the Dubai-based
reader.

### Airport lounge access (Visa Infinite)

No provider or visit cap is published on the issuer page. At the AED 200 walk-in
equivalent, four flown trips a year would be **AED 800/year** — verify the provider and
cap with your relationship manager before counting it.

### Airport transfers, golf, courier

Up to 4 UAE airport transfers a year, free golf at participating UAE courses (40% off
premium courses globally), and up to 12 local courier runs a year — none with a
published cap-and-market-price pairing we'd stand behind, so no AED figure. Real
conveniences for the holder they fit; **AED 0** for everyone else.

### Quarterly AED 5,000 cashback draw

A draw, not a benefit — odds unpublished.
**Value to me: AED 0 for planning purposes.**

**Total package:** the countable items — valet plus the provisional lounge line — sum to
roughly **AED 1,400–4,600/year** depending on utilisation and city, against the
AED 1,575 fee. An Abu Dhabi-based holder who works the valet ration clears the fee on
perks alone; a Dubai-based holder probably doesn't, and is back to the honest position
stated throughout — the relationship, not the card, is the product.

## Watch out for

- **The relationship is the product.** Standalone, AED 1,575 for a 2% Plus Points earner is
  uncompetitive. The card only pencils out if you are already drawing real value from the wider
  Priority Banking package — preferential FX on transfers, fee waivers, RM access, in-branch
  service.
- **Salary-transfer clawback.** The card carries a salary-transfer requirement; expect clawback
  exposure on any joining incentives and the loss of preferential pricing if you change jobs or
  leave the UAE inside tenure.
- **1.99% FX on card spend.** Priority Banking's preferential FX rates apply to outbound transfers
  and currency exchange — not to card transactions. For routine non-AED spend, pair the card with
  a lower-FX product.
- **Fee waiver is discretionary.** Some Priority customers see the AED 1,575 waived at the bank's
  discretion; do not budget on it. Confirm with your RM.
- **Compare across relationships.** Mashreq Gold, ADCB Privilege Club and FAB Elite all offer
  comparable wealth-relationship Visa Infinites — the decision typically follows your primary
  banking, not the other way round.

## Bottom line

A relationship product, not a standalone card. If you are already inside ENBD Priority
Banking, the Visa Infinite is the default in-ecosystem card — it aligns with the
wealth-management relationship, carries the standard Visa Infinite chrome and brings
ENBD-specific perks (Abu Dhabi valet, airport transfers, golf, the quarterly draw) that
the rest of the ENBD card range does not. The preferential FX on transfers and the
cross-product fee waivers usually recover the AED 1,575 card fee comfortably for an
active Priority customer. **Apply** if you are already an Emirates NBD Priority Banking
customer. **Skip** if you are not — the card is invitation-only and opening the entire
wealth-management relationship to chase it does not pencil out; compare Mashreq Gold,
ADCB Privilege Club and FAB Elite at the relationship level instead.
