The Emirates NBD Etihad Guest Visa Inspire is the entry-tier ENBD-Etihad
co-brand. It earns Etihad Guest Miles directly into the cardholder's
loyalty account and is targeted at salaried residents in the AED 12,000+
monthly band.

## What you actually get

Earn is 1 Etihad Guest Mile per AED 1 across all categories. Welcome
bonus is 60,000 Etihad Guest Miles, with the qualifying mechanic
(activation, minimum spend, or both) to be verified against the current
issuer page. Annual fee is AED 735. FX fee is 0%, which is a notable
benefit on a sub-AED-1,000-fee card. Salary transfer is not required;
minimum monthly salary AED 12,000.

## Who should open it

The card matches a household earning AED 12,000–25,000 a month that takes
one or two Etihad flights a year — typically commuter routes (AUH–CAI,
AUH–BLR, AUH–LHR) where the 60,000-mile welcome can fund a single
one-way upgrade or a short-haul reward ticket. It works as a "buy the
welcome bonus" card for Etihad loyalists who don't want to step up to
the AED 1,500+ fee tier.

## Where it falls short

The 1x flat earn is uncompetitive for ongoing spend; once the welcome
bonus is earned, the card sits in the drawer for most rational holders.
Etihad Guest's redemption table has fewer sweet spots than Skywards or
Qatar Privilege Club, and devaluation risk applies the same as everywhere
else.

A clean welcome-bonus product; not a card to spend on long-term.
