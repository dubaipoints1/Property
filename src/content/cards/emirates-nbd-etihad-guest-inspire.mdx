The Emirates NBD Etihad Guest Visa Inspire is the entry tier in ENBD's Etihad
co-brand line. It earns Etihad Guest Miles directly into the cardholder's
loyalty account at the AED 12,000-a-month salary bar, with no salary transfer
required — the most accessible direct Etihad Guest earner on a UAE Visa.

<HotTip>
The Inspire matches the FAB Etihad Guest Infinite's headline partner earn —
**7 Etihad Guest Miles per AED 10** on Etihad-booked airfare, hotels and dining
— at a fraction of the salary bar and the annual fee. The trade-off lives in
everything-else earn (**4 miles per AED 10** here versus the Infinite's 3.5 on
domestic and 6 on international) and in the absence of an Accelerator opt-in.
For a holder whose Etihad spend is concentrated in airfare and dining and
whose general spend is modest, the Inspire is the cleaner buy.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-etihad-guest-inspire" />

The fee structure is unusual: **AED 1,575** joining fee in year one, dropping
to **AED 735** annual fee from year two — both VAT-inclusive. Read the year-one
running cost as AED 1,575, not AED 735. The **1.99%** FX fee is below the
2.49% norm on most premium UAE Visa Infinite cards in market. Minimum salary
is **AED 12,000 a month** and salary transfer is not required.

## Key conditions to know

<dl class="dp-conditions">
  <dt>Welcome bonus mechanics</dt>
  <dd>Up to 60,000 Etihad Guest Miles welcome, posted on activation and qualifying spend per the ENBD product page. A 60,000-mile balance funds a saver-rate short-haul Business one-way (e.g. AUH → BAH / DOH) or a substantial chunk of a longer GuestSeat upgrade.</dd>

  <dt>Silver-tier fast-track</dt>
  <dd>One return Etihad flight within the qualifying window triggers Etihad Guest Silver-tier status — priority check-in, +5kg baggage allowance, and a reduced GuestSeat bid threshold. Unusual for an entry-tier co-brand at this salary bar.</dd>

  <dt>Tier Mile earn</dt>
  <dd>Up to 2.5 Etihad Tier Miles per AED 10 spent, capped at 20,000 Tier Miles per year. Card spend cannot satisfy the from-flying minimum on any Etihad Guest tier (10,000 for Silver, 20,000 for Gold, 40,000 for Platinum) — see the <a href="/airlines/etihad-guest/">Etihad Guest programme guide</a>.</dd>

  <dt>Redemption voucher</dt>
  <dd>25% Etihad Guest Miles redemption-discount voucher on every AED 100,000 of annual spend, capped at 2 vouchers per year — useful for stretching a long-haul Business redemption further if you hit the spend bar.</dd>

  <dt>Lounge access</dt>
  <dd>Complimentary access to 1,000+ airport lounges worldwide via the Visa Airport Companion app. Verify current visit cap and guesting policy on the issuer page before relying on it for a specific trip.</dd>

  <dt>Eligibility</dt>
  <dd>Minimum salary AED 12,000/month; salary transfer not required; UAE residency required; salaried employment.</dd>
</dl>

## Watch out for

- **Year-one joining fee.** AED 1,575 in year one is materially higher than
  the AED 735 annual-fee headline; budget the full first-year cost when
  modelling the welcome bonus return.
- **4 miles per AED 10 on everything else.** Outside Etihad airfare, hotels
  and dining the earn drops to 4 miles per AED 10 — roughly 0.4 miles per AED
  spent. A flat cashback card pays back more on general spend.
- **Tier Mile cap.** Card spend builds Tier Miles only up to 20,000 a year,
  and each Etihad Guest tier carries a from-flying floor (10,000 for Silver,
  20,000 for Gold, 40,000 for Platinum) that only flown Etihad segments
  satisfy. You cannot card-spend your way to status.
- **Etihad Guest is a mid-pack programme.** Redemption sweet spots are
  thinner than Skywards or Qatar Privilege Club; devaluation risk applies
  the same as everywhere else.

## Is it worth it?

For an Abu Dhabi-based or northern-emirate UAE resident who flies Etihad once
or twice a year, earns AED 12,000-plus a month, and doesn't want to commit
to a Visa Infinite annual fee: yes, the Inspire is the most accessible direct
Etihad Guest earner on a UAE Visa. The 60,000-mile welcome funds a short-haul
Business one-way at saver rate (~50,000–88,000 miles depending on route), the
Silver fast-track is a genuine perk at this salary bar, and the 18-month
activity-reset on Etihad Guest Miles means a small monthly card transaction
keeps the balance alive indefinitely — see the
[Etihad Guest programme guide](/airlines/etihad-guest/) for the full Bronze →
Silver → Gold → Platinum → Emerald tier ladder.

For a Dubai-based reader whose flying is primarily on Emirates metal, this is
the wrong card — your earning per dirham is higher on an [ENBD or FAB Skywards
co-brand](/airlines/skywards/) and your tier path is more accessible. For a
salary at AED 30,000-plus who'll spend AED 100,000-plus a year on the card,
the [Emirates NBD Etihad Guest Visa Elevate](/cards/emirates-nbd-etihad-guest-elevate/)
or [FAB Etihad Guest Infinite](/cards/fab-etihad-guest-infinite/) earn more
per dirham and carry stronger lounge and fast-track perks.

## Bottom line

The most accessible direct Etihad Guest earner on a UAE Visa — same headline
partner earn as the premium siblings, at the AED 12,000 salary bar, with a
60,000-mile welcome and a Silver fast-track that no other entry-tier Etihad
co-brand offers. **Apply** if Etihad is your home airline, your salary clears
AED 12,000 a month, and you'll take one return Etihad segment within the
qualifying window. **Skip** if you live in Dubai and fly Emirates more than
Etihad, or if your salary and spend pattern justify stepping up to the
Elevate or FAB Infinite tier.
