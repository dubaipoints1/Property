<HotTip>
noon Credits are not AED — they are a captive currency. A 20% return on a noon Food order
is genuine 20% off your next noon Food order; it is not cash, statement credit, miles or
points transferable elsewhere. The economics work if you would shop on noon anyway. They do
not work as a general cashback card — the off-platform rate is just 1%.
</HotTip>

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-noon-one" />

The card is free for life — AED 0 annual fee, no spend threshold, no clawback. The cost to
watch is a **1.99% foreign-currency fee**: most noon purchases settle in AED, but anything
through a non-AED merchant — or a noon site billed in another GCC currency — picks the fee
up. All published fees include 5% VAT.

## Welcome bonus breakdown

<WelcomeBonusCallout card="emirates-nbd-noon-one" />

The welcome stack runs to three pieces: **AED 500 in noon Credits** for **AED 5,000** of
spend in the first **60 days**, plus a **AED 250 YOUGotaGift HappyYOU Card** on issue, plus
a **1-year free noon One membership** (50% off thereafter). The AED 500 + AED 250 = AED
750 on AED 5,000 of qualifying spend is roughly a 15% return on the opening window — strong
by UAE no-fee-card standards. Critically, the welcome is **new-to-bank or new-to-card
only**: existing Emirates NBD cardholders are excluded and receive nothing for opening it.

## Perks worth using

The Noon One is a free-for-life card, so the per-perk maths is a question of
how much annual return the noon ecosystem actually delivers — not a
break-even against a renewal fee. We've put a per-benefit Value to me on each
so you can see where the AED comes from before applying:

### AED 500 noon Credits welcome (new-to-bank/card only)

Spend AED 5,000 in the first 60 days and AED 500 in noon Credits hit your noon
wallet — a structured 10% return on the qualifying spend. Treat this at face
value if you'll spend the credits on noon within their validity (you will, if
you shop the platform). **Value to me: AED 500 in year one** — straight credit
to noon checkout.

### AED 250 YOUGotaGift HappyYOU Card on issue

A multi-merchant gift card honoured at a published list of UAE retailers and
F&B partners. Face value redeemable at par if the merchant list covers a brand
you'd spend at anyway. **Value to me: AED 250 in year one** if the merchant
list works for you; **AED 150** if you'd discount the card 40% to a brand you
care less about.

### 1-year free noon One membership (50% off thereafter)

The standalone noon One subscription retails around AED 119/year and bundles
free delivery on noon, NowNow and noon Food, plus member-only pricing. The card
covers a full year. **Value to me: AED 119 in year one** — face-value the
subscription. From year two, the 50% discount is **AED ~60/year** if you renew.

### 20% back on noon Food

The headline category rate. UAE families ordering noon Food twice a week at an
average AED 60 basket: 2 × AED 60 × 4.3 weeks × 12 × 20% = **Value to me:
AED 1,238/year** in noon Credits. Light user (one order a week, AED 50 basket):
**AED 516/year**. The combined AED 2,000/month cap binds only if total
accelerated earn exceeds AED 2,000 — for a noon Food-only user, that's AED
10,000/month of orders, which is not most readers.

### 10% back on NowNow

NowNow is noon's quick-commerce grocery arm. Weekly AED 150 basket: 4.3 × AED
150 × 12 × 10% = **Value to me: AED 774/year** in noon Credits. The AED 2,000
combined monthly cap starts to bite if you stack NowNow + noon Food + noon
retail in the same month — track this if you genuinely live on the platform.

### 5% back on noon, Namshi and supermall

The broader retail rate. Household spending AED 1,500/month across noon retail,
Namshi fashion and supermall purchases: 1,500 × 12 × 5% = **Value to me:
AED 900/year**. This is where the AED 2,000 monthly cap is most likely to
trigger for heavy users — combined accelerated earn above AED 2,000 stops
paying that month.

### 1% off-platform

The base earn outside the noon ecosystem. Monthly off-platform card spend of
AED 3,000 (the rest of household life on a non-noon merchant): 3,000 × 12 × 1%
= **Value to me: AED 360/year** in noon Credits. Below the UAE cashback floor
— this is filler earn, not a reason to use the card for general spend.

For a household that already shops noon weekly across food, NowNow and retail,
the ongoing earn comfortably clears **AED 2,000–3,000/year** in noon Credits,
plus the **AED 869 year-one welcome stack** (AED 500 + AED 250 + AED 119).
**Total package: ~AED 2,900–3,900 in year one against AED 0 fee** — fully
captive to the noon ecosystem. If you'd shop noon anyway, the maths is
unambiguous; if you wouldn't, the credits trap the rewards in a drawer.

## Watch out for

- **Captive-currency payout.** noon Credits redeem only on noon platforms. The 20% on noon
  Food is genuine, but only if you order noon Food. Stop shopping on noon and the balance
  effectively traps the rewards.
- **New-to-bank gate on the welcome.** Existing Emirates NBD cardholders get the earn rates
  but none of the AED 500 + AED 250 + noon One welcome. If you already hold an ENBD card,
  the maths is much weaker.
- **1% off-platform.** Outside the noon ecosystem the card is a 1% cashback — well below
  the UAE average. Carry a flat-rate cashback card for everything that isn't noon.
- **FX fee at 1.99%.** Modest by UAE standards but not zero. The no-annual-fee positioning
  shouldn't be confused with a travel card.

## Bottom line

A clean welcome-bonus product for new-to-bank residents who already live on noon. Spend
AED 2,000+ a month on noon platforms, clear AED 5,000 in the first 60 days, and the AED 500
+ AED 250 + free noon One stack is roughly 15% back on the opening window with strong
ongoing earn on spend you'd be doing anyway. **Apply** if you fit the new-to-bank criteria
and shop on noon weekly. **Skip** if you already bank with Emirates NBD (the welcome
disappears), or if your spend isn't concentrated on noon platforms — the 1% off-platform
rate makes a flat-rate AED-denominated cashback card the cleaner hold.
