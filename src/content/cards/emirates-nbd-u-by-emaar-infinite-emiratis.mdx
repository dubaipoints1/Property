The Emirates NBD U by Emaar Infinite (for Emiratis) is a UAE-national-only
variant of the bank's co-branded Visa Infinite. The earn structure mirrors the
open Infinite — 7.5% back as UPoints on Emaar venues, 1.5% on everything else
— but the price of entry is materially lower: AED 1,575 a year with no joining
fee, against AED 2,625 joining plus AED 1,575 renewal on the version available
to all residents.

<HotTip>
The Emiratis variant's pricing changes the break-even maths. On a 7.5% headline
with no joining fee, the AED 1,575 annual recovers at roughly **AED 21,000 a
year** of qualifying Emaar spend — about AED 1,750 a month, well below the
~AED 35,000 of Emaar spend the open Infinite's AED 2,625 joining fee demands
in year one. For UAE-national households whose discretionary
spend lives inside the Emaar footprint, this is the better-priced
version of the same card.

## Fee summary

<FeeBlock card="emirates-nbd-u-by-emaar-infinite-emiratis" />

The card carries an **AED 1,575 annual fee** (inclusive of VAT) with **no
joining fee** — a meaningful gap against the open Infinite's AED 2,625 joining
+ AED 1,575 renewal. The **foreign-currency fee is 1.99%**, in line with the
UAE Visa Infinite segment. Interest is 3.25% per month (about 39% APR) on any
revolved balance — clear the statement in full each month and the interest is
irrelevant.

## What you get — per-benefit value

ENBD quotes the earn as a percentage back in UPoints, so we take the
percentages at face value; the points-denominated welcome converts at the
2-fils-per-UPoint baseline we use across U by Emaar reviews. The fee to beat
is AED 1,575 a year, with no joining fee.

### Welcome bonus

Up to 25,000 UPoints inside the 90-day qualifying window.
**Value to me: AED 500, one-time** at the 2-fil UPoint baseline — captive to
the Emaar ecosystem, and a third of the first year's fee, not a fee-eraser.
Re-confirm the first-year-free campaign at application; if it's live, year
one's maths improves by the full AED 1,575.

### 7.5% UPoints on Emaar venues

**Value to me: AED 2,700/year** on AED 3,000 a month of genuine Emaar spend
(Dubai Mall retail, Emaar F&B, Reel tickets); **AED 1,350/year** at AED 1,500
a month. Paid as captive UPoints. The fee break-even sits at AED 21,000 a
year of Emaar spend on this line alone.

### 1.5% UPoints on everything else

**Value to me: AED 540/year** on AED 3,000 a month of general spend — but
that's captive currency at a rate a flat AED-cashback card matches in open
money. Route general spend elsewhere; treat this line as AED 0 in practice.

### U by Emaar Platinum status — 20% off Emaar Hospitality F&B and spas

**Value to me: AED 1,200/year** on AED 500 a month of Emaar Hospitality
dining you'd be doing anyway (the discount stacks on top of the 7.5% earn on
the same bill). The suite upgrades (capped at 7 nights a year) and up-to-30%
entertainment discounts are availability- and utilisation-dependent with no
published price baseline — **AED 0 unless used**, real when they land.

### Reel Cinemas BOGO

Up to 3 free tickets (6 in total) per month, conditional on AED 5,000 minimum
monthly card spend, online booking only. At the AED 40–50 typical ticket:
**Value to me: AED 480–600/year** on one paired visit a month; up to roughly
**AED 1,400–1,800/year** at the full 3-ticket monthly cap — provided the
spend condition is met every month.

### Visa Infinite chrome

Visa Airport Companion lounge access at 1,000+ lounges. At the AED 200
walk-in equivalent: **Value to me: AED 800/year** on four flown visits.
Concierge, Grand Drive valet at Dubai Mall and travel insurance carry no
published price baseline — AED 0 unless used.

**Total package:** an Emaar-centred Emirati household — AED 3,000 a month of
Emaar spend, AED 500 a month of Emaar Hospitality dining, a monthly Reel
pairing, four lounge visits — collects roughly **AED 5,200–5,300/year**
against the AED 1,575 fee, plus AED 500 of welcome in year one. Drop the
Emaar spend to AED 1,500 a month and the package falls to roughly
**AED 3,800/year** — still ahead, but the margin is now mostly discounts and
lounge rather than earn. Outside the Emaar footprint the package collapses
to the 1.5% line and the fee is not recovered.

## Watch out for

- **Salary-transfer clawback.** ENBD requires salary transfer at AED 30,000 a
  month and applies a standard clawback if you exit the relationship inside
  the offer tenure. If you might change job or leave the UAE within the next
  twelve months, the welcome bonus and waiver economics may not survive the
  exit. Read the clawback clause before signing.
- **UPoints redeem only inside the Emaar ecosystem.** No airline transfers, no
  hotel-programme conversion, no clean cashback escape. If your travel
  ambitions sit outside Emaar's footprint, a Skywards or hotel-co-brand card
  carries more redemption optionality.
- **1.5% on non-Emaar is thin.** Only marginally above the ENBD Plus Points
  base. Treat this as a single-purpose Emaar-spend card, not an everyday
  earner; pair with a stronger flat-rate cashback card for non-Emaar spend.
- **The break-even still has to be earned.** AED 21,000 a month of qualifying
  Emaar spend is lower than the open Infinite's threshold, but it is not
  trivial. Run a twelve-month audit of your real Emaar spend before applying.

## Bottom line

The better-priced version of ENBD's U by Emaar Infinite, restricted to UAE nationals
earning AED 30,000+ a month. For the household whose discretionary spend lives
inside the Emaar footprint — Dubai Mall regulars, Address loyalists, families routing
their dining and weekend entertainment through the Emaar list — the absence of the AED
2,625 joining fee meaningfully changes the maths, and the Visa Infinite chrome and
automatic U by Emaar Platinum status round out the proposition. **Apply** if you qualify
and your Emaar spend is real. **Skip** if your spend sits outside the Emaar list, if the
salary-transfer commitment to ENBD does not fit the wider relationship, or if your
twelve-month employment horizon makes salary-transfer clawback a live risk.
