The FAB Cashback Card sits in the bank's mid-tier lineup as a daily-spend card
aimed at salaried residents earning at least AED 5,000 a month. It does not
require a salary transfer, which is rarer than the marketing suggests in this
segment.

## What you actually get

The earn structure is 5 FAB Rewards per AED 1 on dining and shopping and 1 per
AED 1 on everything else. The reward unit is FAB Rewards rather than direct
cashback, but they redeem inside the FAB Mobile app at a rate that works out
to roughly AED 0.50 cashback per 100 points — call it ~2.5% in the bonus
categories and ~0.5% on the floor. Welcome offer at time of verification:
AED 1,000 Amazon Gift Card on AED 10,000 spend in the first 30 days, or
AED 500 on AED 5,000. The annual fee is AED 2,500 with a first-year waiver,
and a minimum prior-month spend of AED 3,000 is required to earn any rewards
at all that cycle. FX fee is 3%, which is at the high end — this is not a card
to take abroad.

## Who should open it

The card matches a household running AED 5,000–15,000 monthly on dining,
groceries and general shopping, with someone employed on a stable salaried
contract. Salary transfer is not required, so it slots in alongside an existing
primary-bank relationship. If you are inside the first 24 months of UAE
residency and still building AECB history, this is an accessible entry point
into FAB's ecosystem.

## Where it falls short

The AED 3,000 minimum-spend gate is the loud asterisk: any month you slip
below it, your earnings zero out — not pro-rated, not deferred. The 3% FX fee
makes it unsuitable for international spending of any volume. And the FAB
Rewards redemption flow is more friction than a true cashback card; readers
who want a statement-credit experience should look at Mashreq Cashback or
HSBC Cash+ instead.

A sensible cashback choice for FAB's existing customers; not a reason to switch
banks.
