UAE expats hear "salary transfer" in three different conversations — with
HR, with a bank's relationship manager, and on a credit-card application
form — and the term means a slightly different thing in each. The
underlying plumbing is the **Wages Protection System (WPS)**, a Central
Bank-mandated payroll rail that has run since 2009. The card-eligibility
implication sits on top of it.

This guide is part of the [DubaiPoints Expat Starter
track](/guides/expat-starter/) and complements the [UAE banking
basics](/guides/expat-starter-banking-basics/) piece.

## The Wages Protection System

WPS is the route every private-sector UAE employer must use to pay
salaries. MoHRE (the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation)
operates the rules; the Central Bank operates the payment rail. Your
employer uploads a Salary Information File to its bank, the bank
debits the employer's payroll account, and your nominated bank
credits your account on the agreed pay day.

Three points matter for the reader:

- **You nominate the receiving bank, not your employer.** HR will ask
  you for an IBAN. That IBAN is your choice — pick the bank whose
  salary-transfer offer and card lineup actually fits your salary
  band, not the bank HR defaults to.
- **The salary credit arrives with a WPS reference.** Banks read that
  reference to confirm the credit is a *salary* (not a transfer from
  another account), which is what unlocks salary-transfer status.
- **Non-compliance is the employer's problem, not yours.** If WPS
  stops crediting on time, MoHRE can freeze new work-permit issuance
  for that employer. Your recourse is MoHRE, not the bank.

## The salary letter (a separate document)

When you apply for a credit card, mortgage, or personal loan, the
bank wants a **salary certificate** (sometimes called a salary
letter) from your employer, no older than 30 days. It states your
gross salary, your role, your joining date, and — critically —
whether your salary is paid via WPS to that bank.

The salary letter is the document version of the WPS truth. Banks
cross-check the letter against the actual credits landing in your
account over the prior three months. A letter that overstates your
salary, even by a few hundred dirhams, will fail underwriting.

## "Salary transfer required" on a card application

This phrase has a specific meaning: to be approved for the card,
your monthly WPS salary credit must arrive at the issuing bank, and
must continue to arrive for the duration of any tenure clawback.

Banks enforce this in two stages:

1. **At application** — they verify three months of salary credits
   on a statement from their own bank, plus the salary certificate.
2. **Ongoing** — if your salary stops arriving (you change job, you
   redirect WPS to another bank), the card terms typically allow the
   bank to close the account, demand the outstanding balance, and
   claw back any welcome bonus paid in the prior 12 months.

clawback clause before you accept a salary-transfer bonus. A
"AED 2,000 cash on transfer" offer with 12-month tenure means the
bank can recover the full AED 2,000 if you switch in month 11. Plan
your switches around the calendar, not on impulse.</p></div>

## "Salary transfer" versus "salary credit"

A few cards (FAB Cashback, some Citi UAE products, the no-salary-
transfer Mashreq lineup) accept what is called a **salary credit**
relationship — meaning you receive your WPS salary at any UAE bank,
prove it with a salary certificate and statements, and the issuing
bank approves the card without insisting it become your salary
bank. The trade-off is usually a lower welcome bonus and a slightly
higher annual fee than the salary-transfer-required version.

This route is worth knowing if your employer's payroll arrangement
locks you to a specific bank, or if you want to keep an existing
salary-transfer offer running while applying for a card at a
different bank.

## What to do this week

1. Confirm with HR which bank your WPS salary is currently routed
   to, and whether you can change it. Most employers process changes
   within one payroll cycle.
2. If you have an active salary-transfer bonus, check the clawback
   tenure on the offer letter. If the 12 months has cleared, you
   are free to switch.
3. If you are applying for a "salary transfer required" card,
   request the salary certificate from HR before the application —
   not after — so the document is current when underwriting checks
   it.
