The Dubai brunch market resettled after the 2022 weekend shift —
the headline brunches now split between Friday (the legacy slot,
still the loudest) and Saturday (quieter, slightly cheaper at
several hotel groups). Covers run AED 295 at the entry end and
AED 795+ at destination properties. The lever that matters most
is not the day; it is which bank card you pay on.

## The brunch market in three bands

- **AED 295–395 / adult, soft beverage.** Mid-tier hotel brunches
  and the lighter four-star options. Often the best
  per-dirham experience if food matters more than the room.
- **AED 495–595 / adult, house beverage.** The mainstream
  five-star band — Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt city properties. Most
  of the bank-card 2-for-1 programmes are aimed here.
- **AED 695–895 / adult, premium beverage.** Atlantis Saffron /
  Bread Street Kitchen, Madinat Jumeirah's destination venues,
  FIVE's headline brunches. *Editor to verify exact current
  covers against each venue's reservations page — Dubai brunch
  pricing is reset roughly twice a year and the 2026 covers
  should be confirmed at the time of publish.*

Children's pricing is usually half-cover for ages 6–12, free
under 6, but cut-offs vary. Several venues have moved to a flat
AED 95–145 children's rate regardless of age.

## Where each bank card actually works

- **FAB Cheers (FAB World Elite, FAB Elite, others).**
  Buy-one-get-one on the main brunch at a rotating list,
  refreshed quarterly. The list is weighted toward Marriott and
  Hilton city properties; Atlantis and Madinat Jumeirah appear
  intermittently. *Editor to verify in the FAB mobile app.*
- **ENBD Dine (Emirates NBD cards including Bonvoy World Elite
  and Visa Infinite).** Similar 2-for-1 pattern; the list
  over-indexes on Marriott Bonvoy properties given the co-brand.
  Bonvoy World Elite is the strongest single card for the reader
  brunching at Marriott venues regularly. *Editor to verify in
  the ENBD app.*
- **The Entertainer (bundled with several UAE cards in 2026).**
  Wider four- and five-star hotel coverage than either bank-card
  programme alone; same "buy one main brunch, get one free"
  shape. Premium-beverage upgrades are not included, and peak
  destination brunches are typically excluded.

## The stacking rules

You cannot stack two 2-for-1s on the same cover — pick the
deepest. You can stack:

- A 2-for-1 with the hotel's own early-bird or Saturday off-peak,
  if the venue publishes it as a separate promo.
- A 2-for-1 with hotel-loyalty point earning on the cash you do
  pay. Marriott Bonvoy and Hilton Honors credit F&B spend at
  participating UAE properties as base points; points credit to
  the post-discount AED amount.
- A card 2-for-1 with that card's own category earn rate on the
  AED actually charged.

What you cannot do is point-redeem the brunch itself and also
claim the 2-for-1 — the 2-for-1 needs a paid base cover.

## The AED maths on a typical booking

Two adults, mainstream five-star, AED 525 / adult, house beverage:

- Cash, no card benefit: AED 1,050.
- FAB Cheers 2-for-1: AED 525 — a clean 50% saving, with the AED
  525 still earning the card's underlying rewards rate.
- Bonvoy World Elite + ENBD Dine 2-for-1 at a Marriott property:
  AED 525, plus base Bonvoy points on the spend, plus the card's
  underlying rewards on the same AED.

The 2-for-1 is the lever. Everything else is rounding.

## What to do this weekend

1. Pick the hotel group you want — Marriott, Hilton, Atlantis,
   Madinat Jumeirah, FIVE.
2. Cross-reference against current FAB Cheers and ENBD Dine
   lists, plus your Entertainer if you have it.
3. Pay on the card that gives the deepest cover discount, not
   the highest base earn rate — the discount is worth multiples
   of the earn-rate gap.
4. Book the first seating; confirm dress code on the venue's
   reservations page before you leave the house.
