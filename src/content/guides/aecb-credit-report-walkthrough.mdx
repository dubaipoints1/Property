The **Al Etihad Credit Bureau (AECB)** is the UAE's single national
credit bureau. Every regulated UAE lender — bank, finance company,
telco that issues post-paid plans — reports to it monthly. When you
apply for a credit card, a personal loan, a mortgage, or a car
loan, the underwriter pulls one document: your AECB report. Your
UK, US, or Indian credit history does not transfer in.

## What's in the report

The AECB credit report has four sections worth reading carefully:

- **Personal information** — Emirates ID, passport details,
  employer, and address as last reported by your banks. Errors here
  are the most common reason a card application is declined for
  "data mismatch". Check it.
- **Credit facilities** — every active and closed card, loan,
  mortgage, and overdraft, with original limit, current balance,
  monthly instalment, and 24 months of payment history.
- **Bounced cheques** — every returned cheque from the prior five
  years, with amount and reason code.
- **Enquiries** — every credit application that triggered a bureau
  pull in the last 12 months. Banks see this and treat a flurry of
  enquiries as a negative signal.

You also get a **credit score** on a scale from roughly 300 to 900.
Bands are usually read as: under 540 high risk, 540–620 medium-
high, 620–700 medium, 700–760 low-medium, 760+ low risk. Banks
publish slightly different cut-offs; what matters is that premium
cards typically require above 700 and prime mortgages above 720.

## Pulling your own report

You can pull your own AECB report online at **aecb.gov.ae** or
through the AECB mobile app. The process:

1. Register with your Emirates ID, mobile number, and a selfie /
   face-match step.
2. Pay the report fee by card. *Editor to verify exact 2026 fee
   against aecb.gov.ae — the figure has been AED 84 for the
   credit report and AED 105 for the report-plus-score bundle in
   prior years.*
3. Download the PDF immediately; it is also emailed to the address
   on your Emirates ID record.

The full report (with score) is the version worth pulling. The
report-only version saves a few dirhams and is useless for
benchmarking against a bank's cut-off.

your own report once a year, and always before any major application
(mortgage, large personal loan). Errors are not rare — duplicated
facilities, closed cards still showing as open, addresses from a
prior employer. Disputes go through the AECB dispute portal and
typically resolve in 20 working days.</p></div>

## What actually affects the score

In rough order of weight:

- **Payment history.** A single missed payment shows for 24 months
  on the facility history and drags the score immediately. Two
  consecutive misses on the same card is the largest single
  controllable hit.
- **Credit utilisation.** The ratio of current balance to credit
  limit, summed across all cards. Banks dislike utilisation above
  60%; mortgage underwriting prefers under 30%.
- **Number of active facilities.** Three to five cards is fine.
  Eleven cards plus three personal loans reads as overextension,
  even if every payment is current.
- **Length of credit history.** A clean 36-month history beats a
  clean 6-month history at the same utilisation. There is no way
  to fast-forward this.
- **Recent enquiries.** Six card applications in a month is a
  signal of distress. Spread applications across quarters.

## Bounced cheques and defaults

A **bounced cheque** is the single most damaging entry on a UAE
credit report. Cheque-bouncing for amounts below AED 200,000 was
de-criminalised in 2022, but the AECB record stays. Banks reading
a bounced cheque on the file will typically decline a premium card
outright for two to three years after the entry.

A **default** — a credit facility written off by the bank — stays
on the report for **five years** from the date of write-off, per
AECB data-retention rules. Settling the underlying debt closes the
account but does not remove the historic entry; what changes is
the status code from "default" to "settled".

## What to do this week

1. If you have been in the UAE for three months or more, pull your
   report at aecb.gov.ae. Read the personal information section
   carefully — flag any wrong address or employer.
2. If anything is wrong, file a dispute through the AECB portal
   before your next credit application.
3. If you have an active card, target a statement-date utilisation
   below 30%. Pay down the balance the day before the statement
   cuts, not after.
